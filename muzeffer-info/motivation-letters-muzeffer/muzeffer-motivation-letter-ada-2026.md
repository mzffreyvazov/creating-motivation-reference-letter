# Statement of Purpose

**April 8, 2026**

Dear Members of the Admissions Committee,

During my internship at 4SİM, Azerbaijan's Centre for the Fourth Industrial Revolution, I built a RAG-based legal chatbot for e-qanun.az, the government's national legal platform. The engineering part was quite manageable, but the main problem underneath it stood out to me: when I searched for a reliable open-source embedding model for Azerbaijani, I couldn't found anything reliable, thus I had to depend on proprietary foreign infrastructure. This gap, which I can define as foundational AI missing for Azerbaijani language is the research problem I want to dedicate my graduate studies to. I am applying to the MSc in Computer Science and Data Analytics at ADA University to build the expertise to address it.

My background sits at the intersection the MSCS/DA program is built around. At Azerbaijan State University of Economics, I developed strong software engineering foundations: data structures, algorithms, databases, full-stack development. In parallel, I sought out the training which my degree did not provide: I completed the Advanced Computer Science Program with Constructor University Germany through IDDA, then an intensive Data Science Program with IDDA, StrategEast, and Sigma Software University, finishing as a Top 11 performer and earning the Oracle Cloud Infrastructure Data Science Professional certification. During my internship at 4SİM, both sides came together: I used my software engineering experience to architect and deploy the system, and AI knowledge to make it work. The CSDA program treats both field (software engineering and AI) as equally serious, and that combination is exactly what I need to grow into.

What draws me specifically to ADA is the research environment available for the students. Dr. Samir Rustamov's work on NLP for Azerbaijani (dialogue management systems, sentiment analysis for agglutinative languages) sits directly at the center of what I want to pursue for my thesis. The BP-funded Big Data Research Center, the first of its kind in Azerbaijan, is where I want to ground that research in problems that actually matter for my country. During the GWU residency, access to high-performance computing infrastructure and joint faculty supervision would allow me to scale experiments that are not feasible on standard hardware. 

Azerbaijan's AI Strategy 2025–2028 has created real institutional demand for professionals who can build foundational AI tools, not just deploy existing ones. The embedding gap I encountered at 4SİM is one concrete instance of that deficit. The CSDA program,  with its dual degree, research infrastructure, and genuine depth in both computer science and data science, is the right environment to develop the expertise to close it.

**Sincerely,**

**Muzaffar Eyvazov**
