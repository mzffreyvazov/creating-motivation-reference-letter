# Letter of Intent — InterMaths Erasmus Mundus Joint Master

Dear Evaluation Committee,

When I was preparing for my university entrance exams, I couldn't afford multiple tutors, so I decided to study entirely on my own. That constraint turned out to be a gift: to learn effectively, I needed to understand how learning itself works. I started researching memory, cognition, and study techniques, and this curiosity has never left me. Recently, I read *How People Learn* by Nick Shackleton-Jones, which challenged the conventional separation between rational and emotional thinking, suggesting that our brain might operate primarily through affective responses. What fascinated me was not just the neuroscience but the formalism it lacked: I kept wondering whether these biological learning mechanisms could be described mathematically than mere metaphor, and whether such descriptions could reveal new principles and architectures for artificial neural networks. InterMaths' Stochastics for Biological and Artificial Neural Networks track in Nice is the programme where I can finally pursue that question with mathematical rigour.

My Computer Science degree gave me fluency in building AI systems: during my internship at Azerbaijan's Centre for the Fourth Industrial Revolution, I developed RAG-based AI applications for government platforms, worked with embedding models, and deployed ML pipelines. Outside my degree, I completed two competitive programs: the Digital Azerbaijan Talent School's Advanced Computer Science track, organised by IDDA and Constructor University, where I focused on advanced algorithms and data structures and finished in the top 10; and an intensive Data Science Program by IDDA, StrategEast, and Sigma Software University, where I was a Top 11 performer and earned an OCI Data Science Professional certification. But the course that shaped my trajectory most was Numerical Methods. It was one of my favourite courses precisely because it sat at the intersection of mathematics and computation: I implemented every algorithm we studied in Python, used Matplotlib to build regression visualisations my professor later adopted for her lectures, and created mathematical animations of the bisection and Newton-Raphson methods using the Manim library. I also contributed Python implementations for her upcoming textbook. That experience showed me that I learn mathematics most deeply by formalising it computationally, and it left me wanting more, at a far deeper level.

This is exactly what InterMaths offers. The programme's design, building rigorous applied mathematics foundations through Functional Analysis, PDEs, and Dynamical Systems in L'Aquila before moving to scientific computing and probability theory in Hamburg, mirrors how I want to develop: mathematical language first, then application. For Year 2, the Nice track is uniquely compelling. No other programme I have found fuses biological neural networks and artificial neural networks through a single stochastic framework. Stochastic Calculus with Applications to Neuroscience would give me the formal tools, Itô SDEs, diffusion processes, integrate-and-fire models, to mathematise the biological questions I have been asking informally since my self-study days. Stochastics for Machine Learning would connect those same tools to SGD theory and mean-field limits of deep networks, bridging brain and machine. And working alongside faculty like Prof. Patricia Reynaud-Bouret at the NeuroMod Institute and Dr. Etienne Tanré, a former member of the Human Brain Project, would place me in a research environment where these questions are actively being answered.

My goal after InterMaths is to pursue doctoral research at the intersection of stochastic methods, computational neuroscience, and neural network theory. The programme would give me the rigorous mathematical vocabulary to formalise the intuitive questions I have been carrying since I first asked how learning works, and to contribute to understanding how biological learning principles can inform next-generation AI. I bring to the cohort hands-on AI engineering experience, a genuine interdisciplinary curiosity, and the discipline of someone who has always learned by building. I would be honoured to join InterMaths and develop these ideas within its community.

Muzaffar Eyvazov
