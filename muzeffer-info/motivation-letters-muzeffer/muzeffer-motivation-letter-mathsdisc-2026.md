# Letter of Intent — MATHS-DISC Erasmus Mundus Joint Master

Dear Evaluation Committee,

Throughout my Computer Science degree, I was always drawn to the courses that sit at the intersection of mathematics and programming: Numerical Methods, Optimization Techniques, Algorithm Analysis. But it was Numerical Methods that changed how I think about learning. I started implementing the algorithms in Python, building visualisations in Matplotlib and animating the bisection and Newton-Raphson methods with the Manim library. Something clicked. The mathematics I had studied abstractly in calculus and linear algebra suddenly had texture. I could see why an iterative method converges and watch error shrink across iterations. That intersection, where mathematical abstraction becomes something you can build and test through doing, is where my thinking comes alive. It left me wanting more: more mathematical depth, more rigour, more of that feeling.

Since then, my path has taken me deeper into the engineering side of AI. During my internship at Azerbaijan's Centre for the Fourth Industrial Revolution, I built a RAG-based legal chatbot for the national law platform e-qanun.az, engineered automated PDF dashboard generation from raw data, and developed AI-powered tools for institutional use. Alongside my degree, I have fine-tuned language models on Google Cloud's Vertex AI, built prediction pipelines with scikit-learn, and deployed full-stack AI applications.

I know how to build with these tools: assemble pipelines, connect APIs, get outputs. But the more I build, the more I want to go deeper. The modelling, simulation, and optimisation theory that sits underneath these tools is a side of mathematics I have only touched the surface of, and it is exactly what I want to explore further. I want to move beyond applying algorithms to understanding the mathematical foundations that drive them: to design approaches, not just select them from a library.

This is exactly what MATHS-DISC offers. The programme does not just teach machine learning. It teaches the mathematics that makes machine learning work: modelling, simulation, and optimisation in data-rich environments. And it connects those foundations to real industrial and societal challenges. That bridge between mathematical depth and applied problem-solving is what I have been looking for, and it is what sets MATHS-DISC apart from conventional data science programmes.

Within the programme, the Scientific Machine Learning track aligns most closely with where I want to grow. At BUW, the focus on hybrid ML methods and the Interdisciplinary Center for Machine Learning and Data Analytics offer a rigorous, research-grounded approach to scientific ML. At UGA, the pipeline from mathematical optimisation to state-of-the-art ML algorithms, combined with access to EFELIA-MIAI and the broader Grenoble AI ecosystem, provides an equally compelling environment. What draws me to the SML track specifically is its emphasis on developing AI systems that are not just powerful but mathematically grounded: safe, reliable, and interpretable. The Semester 4 industry internship is where I see everything coming together: mathematical training applied to real modelling challenges with partners like Bayer or within the Grenoble Technopole.

After MATHS-DISC, I want to gain industry experience at companies where mathematical modelling and machine learning meet real-world challenges: the kind of environments the programme's own partners represent. Working alongside professionals who apply simulation, optimisation, and scientific ML at scale is where I see myself growing after graduation.

But that is not the end goal. I want to bring that experience back to Azerbaijan. My country's digital transformation is accelerating, and there is a growing need for professionals who understand the mathematics behind AI: people who can design systems, steer technical decisions, and set direction with real depth. I want to be one of those people. The combination of MATHS-DISC's training and top-level industry experience is how I plan to get there.

I bring to the cohort hands-on engineering experience, genuine curiosity about mathematical foundations, and the independence of someone who has always learned by building. I am ready for what MATHS-DISC offers, and I know what I want to do with it.

Muzaffar Eyvazov
