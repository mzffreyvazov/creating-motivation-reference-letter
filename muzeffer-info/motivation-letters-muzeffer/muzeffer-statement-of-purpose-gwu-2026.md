# Statement of Purpose

**May 14, 2026**

Dear Members of the Admissions Committee,

The computer science problem I want to study became clear while I was building a retrieval-augmented generation chatbot for e-qanun.az, Azerbaijan's national legal platform, during my internship at 4SIM Azerbaijan, the Centre for the Fourth Industrial Revolution. The system made legal documents easier to search, but the underlying limitation was more important: I could not find a reliable open-source embedding model for Azerbaijani. For sensitive legal data, dependence on proprietary foreign infrastructure was not a small inconvenience; it showed me that Azerbaijan still lacks foundational AI tools for its own language. That realization is why I am pursuing the ADA-GWU dual-degree program, and why the GWU M.S. in Computer Science is essential to my path.

At Azerbaijan State University of Economics, I built a strong foundation in software engineering, algorithms, databases, and full-stack development. Beyond my formal univeristy education, I completed the Advanced Computer Science Program organized by IDDA with Constructor University, then the Data Science Program by IDDA, StrategEast, and Sigma Software University, where I was recognized as a Top 11 performer and earned the OCI Data Science Professional certification. Then, during my internship at 4SIM, I applied both sides: engineering reliable systems and making them intelligent through AI.

Within the dual-degree program, GWU's M.S. in Computer Science is where I hope to gain the theoretical and systems training behind the problems I want to solve. Its breadth across machine intelligence, database and information retrieval systems, algorithms, distributed computing, and software engineering matches the technical layers behind modern AI infrastructure. I am especially interested in natural language processing, information retrieval, and trustworthy AI. Professor Rebecca Hwa's work in NLP and machine learning, and Professor Abdou Youssef's work in NLP, search, and model representation, closely align with my interest in low-resource language technologies. The thesis option would allow me to investigate open-source Azerbaijani embedding models or retrieval systems under GW faculty supervision, potentially with ADA or professional co-advisors.

The fully funded ADA-GWU structure is meaningful because the two degrees serve complementary purposes. ADA's M.S. in Data Analytics grounds my work in Azerbaijan's applied needs, while GWU's M.S. in Computer Science provides the depth needed to build the systems behind those applications. My long-term goal is to contribute to Azerbaijan's AI Strategy 2025-2028 by developing open-source NLP infrastructure, retrieval tools, and trustworthy AI systems for public and private institutions. The GWU component would give me the computer science discipline to make that contribution real.

Sincerely,

**Muzaffar Eyvazov**
