# Course Syllbuses for the Courses of Referee Teachers

### **1. Artificial Intelligence**

1.  Introduction to Artificial Intelligence: history, stages of development, and ethical aspects.
2.  Claude Shannon's approach to information, data, and knowledge concepts. Data representation and problem description.
3.  Search algorithms: depth-first, breadth-first, and heuristic approaches.
4.  Data structures and knowledge representation models and problems in artificial intelligence.
5.  Fuzzy Logic and decision-making under uncertainty.
6.  Expert systems engineering and knowledge engineering.
7.  Introduction to Neural Networks. Biological and mathematical foundations.
8.  Deep Learning and its main architectures.
9.  Natural Language Processing (NLP) and speech/text understanding.
10. Genetic algorithms and evolutionary approaches.
11. Intelligent agents, single and multi-agent systems.
12. Main methods of Machine Learning (supervised, unsupervised, reinforcement).
13. Decision-making and planning problems in intelligent systems.
14. Hybrid systems: neural networks + fuzzy logic + genetic algorithms.
15. Real-world applications of Artificial Intelligence: banking sector, logistics, smart city, Industry 4.0, healthcare.

---

### **2. Cloud Technologies**

1.  Introduction to Cloud Technologies.
2.  Cloud computing service models.
3.  Cloud computing deployment models.
4.  Purpose of the Google Drive application. Using the text editor in Google Drive.
5.  Preparing spreadsheets and presentations in Google Drive.
6.  What is Amazon Web Services (AWS)?
7.  Initial setup in AWS and one-year free usage.
8.  IAM Identities (Credentials) in AWS.
9.  EC2 Service and its features in AWS.
10. S3 (Simple Storage Service) in AWS.
11. AWS CloudWatch.
12. General features of AWS DynamoDB.
13. Amazon SNS (Simple Notification Service) and SQS (Simple Queue Service).
14. AWS Lambda.
15. Amazon Kinesis.

---

### **3. Numerical Methods**

1.  Approximate calculations.
2.  Calculation of values of elementary functions.
3.  Numerical solution methods for single-variable non-linear algebraic equations.
4.  Solving non-linear equations using secant, tangent, and combined methods.
5.  Numerical solution methods for systems of linear algebraic equations.
6.  Finite differences of various orders, divided differences, and their properties.
7.  The problem of function approximation. Lagrange interpolation polynomial.
8.  Newton's interpolation formulas.
9.  Construction of empirical formulas.
10. Solution of the numerical differentiation problem.
11. Numerical integration formulas.
12. Trapezoidal and Simpson's rules, and estimation of their errors.
13. Approximate solution methods for ordinary differential equations and systems of equations.
14. Numerical solution methods for boundary value problems for ordinary differential equations.
15. Numerical solution methods for boundary value problems for partial differential equations.
