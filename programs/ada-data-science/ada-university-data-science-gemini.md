## **1\) Executive Summary**

The Master of Science in Computer Science and Data Analytics at ADA University represents a strategic academic infrastructure designed to facilitate the high-level technical transition of Azerbaijan’s burgeoning technology sector. This five-semester dual-degree program, established through a high-level five-year agreement with George Washington University’s School of Engineering and Applied Science, aims to bridge the gap between regional software engineering practices and international standards of data-driven scientific inquiry.1 The program is specifically architected for aspiring professionals who seek a synthesis of advanced computational theory and the applied mechanics of big data, offering a unique residency model that splits time between the sophisticated academic environment of Baku and the global technological hub of Washington, D.C..1

A defining characteristic of this graduate track is its reliance on a rigorous fully-funded model for Azerbaijani nationals, supported by the national "State Program" and industrial partnerships with BP, which underscores the program's role as a national priority for human capital development.1 This funding mechanism not only covers tuition but also addresses the significant logistical requirements of international study, including travel and cost-of-living stipends for the American residency phase.1 The curriculum is deeply rooted in the foundational pillars of computer science—algorithms, systems architecture, and software paradigms—while branching into specialized domains such as machine learning, natural language processing, and computer vision.1

The program optimizes for a candidate profile that demonstrates a rare combination of mathematical precision, research potential, and a commitment to civic responsibility.8 Selection criteria are strictly merit-based, requiring a high undergraduate GPA and a successful performance in both an internal technical examination and the State Examination Center (SEC) master’s test.1 By maintaining a small cohort size of approximately 20 students, the university ensures a high degree of faculty interaction and personalized research mentorship, preparing graduates for dual trajectories in either elite industry roles or advanced doctoral research.4

Graduates emerge with a dual diploma—a Master of Science in Computer Science from GWU and a Master of Science in Data Science and Analytics from ADA—providing them with an unparalleled competitive edge in the global labor market.4 The program's integration with the Big Data Research Center at ADA SITE further enhances this by providing a direct pipeline to real-world industrial projects, ensuring that the theoretical knowledge acquired in the classroom is immediately applicable to solving complex organizational challenges in the energy, finance, and public sectors.12

## **2\) What the Program Optimizes For (Selection Signals)**

The selection philosophy of the MSCS/DA program at ADA University is governed by the institution's "ADA 2025" strategic vision, which seeks to cultivate "highly intellectual solution providers" capable of navigating the multifaceted challenges of a globalized economy.9 This optimization extends beyond mere academic competence; it signals a search for individuals who possess a "growth mindset" characterized by persistence, adaptability, and "grit".10 The university explicitly values "research potential," prioritizing candidates who can articulate a clear motivation for developing innovative solutions through rigorous study and who can demonstrate this through previous academic publications, software innovations, or complex work experience.1

Ethical leadership and social responsibility serve as critical "selection signals" during the evaluative process. The program seeks to attract candidates whose professional goals align with the public good, often looking for alignment with the "Taghiyev Legacy" of civic engagement.8 This is reflected in the holistic review of the Statement of Purpose, where applicants must prove they are not only technically proficient but also possess a sense of "honor, integrity, and transparency".9 The program emphasizes "intellectual potential" alongside a "career focus," meaning the admissions committee looks for a definitive trajectory where the dual degree serves as a catalyst for significant impact within the Azerbaijani or global ecosystem.1

Furthermore, the program optimizes for candidates with a "strong command of English," as the entirety of the instruction is conducted in English to prepare students for the international collaborative environment they will enter at GWU.3 The ideal candidate is expected to be a "close collaborator" and an "effective communicator," as the curriculum and research projects often involve teamwork and interdisciplinary approaches.10 In essence, the program signals for a "sound talent base" that is ready to be transformed into creative thinkers and global leaders who can mobilize others and manage the inherent ambiguity of the modern tech landscape.10

## **3\) Curriculum & Academic Experience**

The academic odyssey of the MSCS/DA program is structured across five intensive semesters, ensuring a comprehensive immersion into both the theoretical underpinnings of computation and the practical nuances of data science.1 The progression is designed to build a solid foundational knowledge in the first year before transitioning to highly specialized domains and a year-long research commitment.1

### **Program Structure and Residency**

The residency model is one of the most distinctive aspects of the curriculum. Students spend the first three semesters at the ADA University campus in Baku, where the focus is on the "Major Core" and establishing a research direction.1 This is followed by two semesters—the Summer and Fall of the second year—at George Washington University’s School of Engineering and Applied Science (SEAS) in Washington, D.C..1 This phase is critical as it grants students access to "world-class campus labs, centers, and institutes," providing a different pedagogical perspective and networking opportunities within the US tech ecosystem.1 The final semester is spent back in Baku for the completion and defense of the Master’s Thesis.5

### **The Master’s Thesis and Capstone**

The Master’s Thesis is the definitive capstone of the program, requiring 18 ECTS credits (split 6 in the first part and 12 in the second).1 It is not a purely academic exercise but a rigorous investigation into a research or professional topic intended to prepare graduates for high-level research roles.1 A unique feature of this program is that the thesis is "jointly supervised" by faculty from both ADA University and George Washington University, ensuring that the research meets international peer-review standards and addresses problems of global relevance.1 Students are expected to apply what they have learned—be it in "Software Paradigms" or "Machine Learning"—to develop a substantial software artifact or a research paper.18

### **Practical Components and Distinctive Features**

Practical engagement is woven into the curriculum through the "Experiential Learning" philosophy.19 At ADA, students utilize the "Big Data Research Center" to engage in initiatives involving the applications of AI and techno-entrepreneurship.12 During the GWU residency, students have access to the "Lovelace" High-Performance Computing cluster, allowing them to perform parallel/distributed data processing that standard workstations cannot handle.20 The program also includes "Employability Week," an intensive program where students directly engage with employers, attending sessions on "AI Readiness" and "Intrapreneurial Thinking".22 Furthermore, the program’s partnership with companies like BP provides a direct link to "real business cases" and practical workplace expectations.22

## **4\) Research Ecosystem (Labs/Groups/Faculty)**

The research ecosystem of the School of Information Technologies and Engineering (SITE) at ADA University is a "hub of scientific discovery" that promotes the fusion of academic theory and industrial application.12 The research culture is anchored by several key labs and highly specialized faculty members whose work addresses the unique technological and linguistic needs of the region.13

### **Key Faculty and Research Directions**

* **Dr. Abzatdin Adamov (Dean of SITE)**: As a central figure in the university's research strategy, Dr. Adamov focuses on High-Performance Computing (HPC) and Big Data analytics. His work includes "Large-scale Data Modeling in Hive" and the study of distributed query processing using MapReduce and Tez.24 His research on "Machine Learning in Tax Fraud Detection" highlights his interest in applying complex algorithms to societal and economic challenges.24 This is particularly relevant for students interested in the intersection of data science and public policy. 24  
* **Dr. Samir Rustamov (Associate Professor)**: Dr. Rustamov is a leading expert in Artificial Intelligence and Natural Language Processing (NLP), with a specific focus on the Azerbaijani language. His research covers "Dialogue Management Systems" for the banking sector and "Speech Recognition in Flight Simulators".23 His work on "Sentiment Analysis for Agglutinative Languages" is pioneering and provides a unique opportunity for students to work on low-resource language technologies.23 He also explores the "Computational Acceleration of Topology Optimization" using deep learning, showcasing his versatility in applied AI.2325  
* **Dr. Jamaladdin Hasanov (Associate Professor)**: Specializing in Computer Vision and Medical Imaging, Dr. Hasanov’s research has significant implications for the healthcare sector. His recent projects include "Quantum Machine Learning Algorithms for Breast Cancer Classification" and the development of "Sign Language Recognition" systems for Azerbaijani.27 His background bridges 20 years of industrial experience with academic research in image classification and segmentation.2928  
* **Dr. Vugar Musayev (Associate Dean)**: Dr. Musayev focuses on Information Security and "Privacy Preserving Data Mining." His research addresses the multidisciplinary problems of information security within the e-government environment and the integration of European research networks through the EaPConnect project.2412

### **Strategic Research Facilities**

* **Big Data, Data Sciences Instruction and Research Center**: Established in collaboration with BP, this center is the first of its kind in Azerbaijan. It serves as a laboratory for "data-driven strategies" in national policy and innovation, providing students with access to best-in-class software and hardware technologies.13  
* **A-Lab (Innovation Center)**: Functioning as an incubator for start-ups, the A-Lab allows students to commercialize their innovative ideas and develop foundational knowledge about technology entrepreneurship.10 It acts as a bridge between research and the venture capital ecosystem, providing seed funding for successful student projects.10  
* **George Washington University SEAS Labs**: During the residency phase, students gain access to state-of-the-art research laboratories at GWU, where they work under joint supervision on projects related to cloud systems and parallel architectures.1 The "Lovelace" HPC system at GWU supports research projects across all academic units, providing high-speed distributed data processing and research-dedicated storage.20  
* **Technology Competence Center & Cybersecurity Lab**: These facilities are designated under the university’s strategic plan to provide specialized training and research opportunities in collaboration with major tech companies like Microsoft, Cisco, and SAP.10

## **5\) Admissions & Eligibility (Hard Requirements)**

Admissions into the MSc in Computer Science and Data Analytics program are governed by a competitive, multi-stage process that filters for both technical proficiency and intellectual growth potential.1 Because it is a dual-degree program, applicants must meet the standards of both ADA University and George Washington University.1

### **Academic and Background Prerequisites**

A Bachelor's degree in a relevant STEM field—specifically Computer Science, Computer Engineering, Information Technology, or Applied Mathematics—is a non-negotiable requirement.1 Candidates must demonstrate a "strong background" through prior coursework in four key areas: Programming Principles I and II, Discrete Mathematics, and Data Structures and Algorithms.1 Furthermore, applicants must have completed at least two mathematics courses beyond the Calculus level, such as Linear Algebra or Probability and Statistics.1

The GPA requirement is set at a minimum of 3.0 on a 4.0 scale (roughly equivalent to 80/100) based on the last 60 credit hours of undergraduate work.1 Notably, ADA University provides a specific exemption for its own students and alumni, who may apply with a minimum GPA of 2.8.1

### **Language Proficiency Requirements**

Instruction and research are conducted entirely in English, necessitating high proficiency. The following minimum test scores are accepted:

| **IELTS Academic** | 7.0 (One Skill Retake is accepted) 1 |
| **TOEFL iBT** | 100 1 |
| **PTE Academic** | 68 1 |

Waivers are granted to native speakers or those who graduated from English-medium degree programs in countries where English is an official language.1 ADA University alumni and students enrolled in ADA's EAPP who pass with a grade higher than 'B' are also exempt.1

### **Mandatory Documents and Standardized Tests**

The application portfolio must include several critical documents:

* **Statement of Purpose (SoP)**: A document of 300–500 words (some forms specify a 400-word limit) detailing academic and professional goals and alignment with the dual-degree program.2  
* **CV/Resume**: An updated document listing educational background, employment experiences, and relevant skills.1  
* **Academic Transcripts and Diploma**: Notarized and English-translated copies of the Bachelor’s degree; final-year students must provide a graduation expectancy document.2  
* **SEC Master Exam**: A minimum score of 45 is required for citizens of Azerbaijan and those with permanent residence.1  
* **Internal ADA Exam**: A multiple-choice technical exam in English covering programming languages, architecture, algorithms, and networking.1

## **6\) Application Process & Deadlines**

The application process is a four-stage sequential pipeline that requires coordination between national testing bodies, ADA University, and George Washington University.1 It is critical to note that late application rounds may have limited availability, and early submission is recommended for funding purposes.2

### **Key Deadlines for 2026/2027 Intake**

The university operates on two main deadlines, although English scores can often be submitted until the late cutoff.1

* **First Round Deadline**: April 8, 2026\.1  
* **Second Round Deadline**: June 1, 2026\.2  
* **Late Submission for English Scores**: May 1, 2026 (for those applying in the first round) or June 1, 2026 (general cutoff).1  
* **Final Decision Notification**: Typically by late May for early applicants and June for late applicants.2

### **The Application Pipeline**

1. **Stage 1: ADA University Online Application**: Submission of all required documents and payment of a 100 AZN application fee.1  
2. **Stage 2: Technical and SEC Exams**: Completion of the SEC/DIM Master Exam and the ADA Internal technical exam.1  
3. **Stage 3: GWU Online Application**: Candidates nominated after the first two stages are invited to apply to GWU. These nominees are exempt from the GWU application fee.1  
4. **Stage 4: Admission Interview**: Shortlisted applicants attend an evaluative interview in English with the Admission Committee.1

### **Evaluation Rubric**

The Admission Committee utilizes a **holistic review** policy. Beyond standardized scores, the committee evaluates the applicant's "intellectual potential," "career focus," and "readiness to succeed" in an intensive international environment.1 Research potential is a key qualitative factor; the committee looks for evidence of previous research projects or publications during both the document review and the interview phase.1 There is no explicit "rolling admissions" policy, but applications are processed in discrete rounds with fixed interview windows.2

## **7\) Funding / Tuition / Cost**

The program is uniquely structured as a **Fully Funded Dual Degree Program** for Azerbaijani nationals, supported by the national "State Program" to improve international competitiveness and industrial co-venturers.1

### **Tuition and Fees**

For those who do not qualify for state funding (such as international applicants or those who have already used state-funded master’s slots), the annual tuition at SITE is significant.1

* **Student Fee (Mandatory for All)**: 1,500 AZN per year (non-refundable).1  
* **Non-Citizen Graduate Tuition (General Reference)**: Approximately $36,200 USD for the dual-degree program if not covered by the scholarship.37  
* **Azerbaijani Citizen Tuition (Unfunded)**: 16,500 AZN per year.37

### **Scholarships and Funding Coverage**

The **State Program (2022-2028)** and BP cover the following for qualifying Azerbaijani citizens:

* Full tuition at both ADA University and George Washington University.1  
* Round-trip airfare and US visa fees.1  
* A monthly stipend for living costs during the residency in Washington, D.C..1  
* Dormitory accommodation in Washington, D.C..1

### **Cost of Living Guidance**

While in Baku, students are generally responsible for their own living expenses. ADA University does not guarantee housing but cooperates with two student residences; housing is not included in the Baku portion of the funding.38 For the Washington residency, the funding is comprehensive, designed to ensure students can focus entirely on their studies without financial stress.1

## **8\) Career Outcomes**

The MSCS/DA dual degree is designed to create high-value workforce leaders capable of delivering innovative solutions to real-life problems.7 The curriculum's integration with industry and the residency in Washington, D.C., provide a dual-market advantage.1

### **Typical Roles and Industries**

Graduates are prepared for senior engineering and leadership roles in sectors requiring high-performance computing, cloud systems, and data analytics.11 Typical positions include:

* **Data Scientist / Big Data Engineer**: Developing predictive models and managing distributed datasets for large enterprises like BP or state agencies.11  
* **Machine Learning Engineer**: Designing autonomous systems and applying NLP technologies to banking or customer service industries.23  
* **Research Scientist**: Pursuing advanced careers in academia or research-focused roles in private sectors.1  
* **Cloud Architect / DevOps Engineer**: Managing the computational stack from architecture to parallel programming.16

### **Employability and Placement**

The ADA Career Management Center (CMC) reports that 93% of undergraduate and graduate students in technical fields find employment or pursue further study shortly after graduation.3 The university hosts an annual "Career Fair" and "Employability Week" to connect students with over 1,000 partner organizations.22 Notably, graduates of the MSCS/DA program are **not** required to remain in Azerbaijan or work in the country for five years after graduation, allowing them to compete in the global labor market.1 Many graduates leverage the GWU network to find roles in multinational tech firms or continue to doctoral studies in high-ranking US or European universities.11
