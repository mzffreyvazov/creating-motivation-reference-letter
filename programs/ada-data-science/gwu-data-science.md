## 1) Executive Summary (5–8 bullets)

- This is a **fully-funded dual-degree program**: graduates earn both a **GWU M.S. in Computer Science** and an **ADA University M.S. in Data Analytics** in roughly 2–2.5 years (5 semesters).
- The program was designed explicitly **to address the growing demand for skilled tech professionals in Azerbaijan**, making it a national-capacity-building initiative — a fact that is powerful for a motivation letter.
- Structure: **3 semesters at ADA in Baku + 2 semesters at GWU's Foggy Bottom campus** in Washington, D.C., with classes also held at the ADA International building in Dupont Circle.
- **Full funding** covers tuition, travel costs, and living expenses in Washington, D.C. — the scholarship is built into the program, not a separate competition.
- Applicants are first screened by ADA University; **only candidates who pass ADA's internal process are then invited to complete the GWU online application** — which is exactly Müzaffar's current stage.
- The GWU component sits within the **School of Engineering & Applied Science (SEAS)**, a research-intensive school with strong AI/ML, cybersecurity, and interdisciplinary computing labs.
- The CS program covers algorithms, AI/ML, distributed systems, security, and software engineering — broad by design, enabling students to specialise via electives and an optional thesis.
- GWU's location in Washington, D.C. gives students proximity to government agencies, think-tanks, and the mid-Atlantic tech corridor.

## 3) Curriculum & Academic Experience

- **Structure:** 30 credit hours, 5 semesters total (3 at ADA, 2 at GWU). Full-time. Thesis and non-thesis options available.

- **Core areas covered (from official program description):**
  - Algorithms and theory
  - Computer architecture, networks, parallel and distributed computing
  - Computer security and information assurance
  - Database and information retrieval systems
  - Machine intelligence and cognition
  - Multimedia, animation, graphics and user interface
  - Software engineering and systems

- **Thesis/Capstone:**
  - Thesis option: coursework replaced by supervised research credits. Thesis advisor must be a GWU CS faculty member (full-time or research faculty). Co-advisors (including external professionals) are permitted.
  - Non-thesis option: additional coursework fills remaining credit hours.

- **Distinctive elements:**
  - Students spend two semesters physically on GWU's campus, using the **Science & Engineering Hall (SEH)** — a state-of-the-art facility with advanced labs, research centers, and institutes.
  - The ADA component complements the GWU CS degree with **Data Analytics**, meaning graduates hold a data-science credential on top of the CS degree — highly relevant to industry roles.
  - Access to GWU's **Motion Capture and Analysis Laboratory (MOCA)**, **Institute for Innovation in Health Computing**, and **Institute for Data, Democracy, and Politics (IDDP)**.
  - MS Placement Exam exists for incoming students to ensure correct course placement.

## 5) Admissions & Eligibility (Hard Requirements)

> **Critical context for Müzaffar:** Since he has already passed the ADA internal selection process, many of the ADA-side requirements (SEC exam ≥45, ADA SoP ≤400 words, diploma/transcripts, CV, photo, national ID) are already submitted. The GWU online application is a separate submission directly to GWU SEAS.

- **Academic:** Bachelor's degree in any field, with coursework in: (a) mathematics — two courses beyond pre-calculus; (b) lab science — one year; (c) CS — prior coursework in a structured language, discrete structures, data structures, or computer architecture. Min. GPA of 3.0/4.0 (or equivalent) on last 60 credit hours. [GWU CS requirements](https://cs.engineering.gwu.edu/master-science-computer-science)

- **Language (for GWU application):**
  - **IELTS:** 6.5 overall, no individual band below 5.5
  - **TOEFL:** 4.0 overall (for exams after Jan 21, 2026); 81 for earlier exams
  - **Duolingo:** 115 or higher
  - **PTE Academic:** 53 overall
  - Scores must not be more than 2 years old.
  - Note: Students with TOEFL below 100 / IELTS below 7.0 / Duolingo below 130 will be required to take an EAP (English for Academic Purposes) course in their first semester at GWU. This does not affect degree completion but adds 3 credits not counting toward the degree.
  - [International Admissions & Language Requirements](https://graduate.engineering.gwu.edu/international-admissions)

- **Documents for GWU online application:**
  - Completed online application form (submitted by the applicant personally — forgeries result in immediate withdrawal)
  - Scanned unofficial transcripts from all institutions (front and back of each page, highest resolution, PDF or DOCX)
  - For international applicants: transcripts in **both English and the original language**. GWU does **not** accept third-party evaluations (e.g., WES).
  - Statement of Purpose: **250–500 words** — must state purpose in undertaking graduate study at GWU; academic objectives, research interests, career goals; related qualifications.
  - CV/Resume (PDF or DOCX)
  - Minimum **two (2) letters of recommendation** from professors, work supervisors, or colleagues who can speak to academic/professional potential
  - Application fee: **$80** (paid by credit card online; fee is non-refundable once submitted)
  - GRE: **optional** (no minimum score; above 50th percentile encouraged if submitted)
  - [Master's Admissions Requirements](https://graduate.engineering.gwu.edu/masters-program-admissions-requirements)

- **References:** Recommenders receive an automated email with a link to submit online. Full name and email address of each recommender required at application time.

## 7) Funding / Tuition / Cost

- **Full funding for ADA-GW partnership participants:** All participants receive full funding covering **tuition fees, travel costs, and living expenses in Washington, D.C.** This is the defining feature of this program — no separate scholarship application is needed; it is part of the program offer.
  - [ADA & GW Partnership Program — funding statement](https://graduate.engineering.gwu.edu/ada-gw-partnership-program)

- **Tuition (for reference/context — covered by the program):**
  - 2026–2027: **$2,195 per credit hour** (on-campus graduate rate)
  - 30-credit MS = approximately **$65,850 in tuition** total
  - Full-time (9 credits/semester): ~$19,755 per semester in tuition alone
  - One-time matriculation fee: $495
  - International student semesterly fee (F-1/J-1): $45/semester
  - [Tuition and Fees 2026–2027](https://graduate.engineering.gwu.edu/tuition-and-fees)

- **Cost of living in D.C. (no official GWU guidance found):**
  - Washington, D.C. is one of the most expensive U.S. cities. Monthly living costs (housing, food, transport) for a student are typically $2,500–$3,500+. Since the program covers living expenses during the 2 D.C. semesters, this is fully mitigated for ADA-GW students.

## 10) Risks, Ambiguities, and To-Confirm Checklist

- **Application coordination:** The GWU page says ADA-selected candidates "will be required to apply to the George Washington University" — but it is not clear whether GWU processes these applications under any special expedited or coordinated review, or whether they go through the standard rolling admissions queue. **Confirm with ADA admissions office** whether they notify GWU in advance, and whether any separate reference letter process applies.

- **SoP word limit discrepancy:** ADA's SoP requirement was ≤400 words. GWU's SoP requirement is 250–500 words. These are **different documents** for different purposes — do not submit the ADA SoP to GWU without adapting it. The GWU SoP must specifically address GWU, its programs, research interests at GWU, and career goals.

- **Transcripts format:** GWU requires transcripts in **both English and the original language** (Azerbaijani). They do **not** accept WES or other third-party evaluations. Confirm that existing notary-certified translations submitted to ADA are in a format GWU will accept (scanned PDFs uploaded directly to the application portal).

- **Application fee:** The $80 fee must be paid. Confirm whether ADA/GWU partnership has any fee waiver arrangement — the standard waivers listed (GW alumni, US military) would not apply. If not waived, budget for this.

- **GRE:** Optional for GWU SEAS MS applicants. Given the program's competitive and funded nature, submitting strong GRE scores (above 50th percentile) could strengthen the application — but is not required. **Decide and plan accordingly.**

- **English scores and EAP risk:** If Müzaffar's IELTS/TOEFL scores are between the minimum threshold and the EAP-trigger threshold (IELTS 6.5–7.0, TOEFL 81–100), he will be admitted but required to take a non-degree EAP course in his first semester at GWU. This is manageable but worth knowing in advance.

- **"Once submitted, no changes"**: GWU explicitly states that once the application is submitted and fee paid, no further changes are possible. Review all materials carefully before hitting submit.

- **Career outcomes page was a 404:** The direct link `careers.seas.gwu.edu/channels/computer-science/` returned a 404 error at time of research. Check [GWU SEAS Graduate Career Services](https://graduate.engineering.gwu.edu/graduate-career-services) directly for updated outcomes data.

- **ADA page for CS + Data Analytics program:** Müzaffar should also review [the ADA program page](https://www.ada.edu.az/en/schools/site/programs/computer-science-and-data-analytics) to understand the integrated curriculum and use ADA-specific curriculum details in the motivation letter.
