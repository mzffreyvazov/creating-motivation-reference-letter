## 1) Executive Summary (5–8 bullets)

- The MSc in Artificial Intelligence and Data Engineering was formally established by Senate decision on **19 July 2024** (decision no. 632/5919) and admitted its first cohort in Spring 2024–2025; it is a very new, rapidly growing programme built on a popular undergraduate track launched in 2022.
- The programme sits inside Ankara University's Engineering Faculty and is closely linked to the separately housed **Artificial Intelligence Institute (YZE)**, which runs its own labs (Data Science & Analytics Lab, Robotics & Automation Lab, AI Applications Lab) and TÜBİTAK-funded industry projects.
- Instruction language is **Turkish**; international students without Turkish C1 will require TÖMER preparation (standard practice for Turkish-medium programmes under Türkiye Scholarships).
- **Hard entry thresholds:** GPA ≥ 2.20/4.00; ALES (quantitative) ≥ 70; foreign language (YDS/YÖKDİL or equivalent) ≥ 55.
- Eligible undergraduate degrees span AI Engineering, Computer Engineering, Software Engineering, EE, Mechatronics, Mathematical Engineering, Control & Automation Engineering, Industrial Engineering, and Mathematics & Statistics.
- The faculty is small (9 academic staff) but research-active across machine learning, bioinformatics/health informatics, NLP, deep learning, optimization, IoT, big data, image security, and autonomous systems — giving good supervision choice for a technically focused applicant.
- Applying via **Türkiye Scholarships (Türkiye Burslari)** is the primary route; the 2026 application window was January 10 – February 20, 2026. For the 2026/2027 intake the window will likely open again in January 2027 — confirm on turkiyeburslari.gov.tr.
- Career outcomes target **AI/data scientist roles** in IT, finance, healthcare, automotive, telecommunications, and defence — sectors for which Ankara (as Turkey's capital and a major government-tech hub) provides strong proximity.

## 3) Curriculum & Academic Experience

- **Structure:** Full-time, 2-year (4-semester) thesis-based master's. Coursework concentrated in semesters 1–2; thesis in semesters 3–4. Students must complete a minimum number of graduate courses (specific credit count not yet published for the MSc, typical Ankara University MSc norm is 7–8 courses + seminar + thesis). Up to 2 courses may be drawn from undergraduate-level courses not previously taken; up to 2 from other postgraduate programmes.

- **Core research/thematic areas (as listed on department Research page):**
  - Machine Learning (supervised/unsupervised models, classification, regression)
  - Deep Learning (neural network architectures, representation learning)
  - Data Mining (tabular, text, audio, image data)
  - Natural Language Processing (dialogue systems, sentiment analysis, machine translation)
  - Bioinformatics (computational biology, omics data, genetic disease diagnosis)
  - Optimization Algorithms (swarm intelligence, evolutionary computation, metaheuristics)

- **Electives (representative, inferred from faculty expertise and BBS course catalogue link):**
  - Big Data Technologies / Graph Mining
  - Cryptography & Data Security / Image Encryption
  - IoT and Data Analytics
  - Autonomous Systems & Fuzzy Logic Control
  - Health Informatics / Medical AI
  - Reinforcement Learning
  - Large Language Models

- **Thesis/Capstone:** Original research thesis is **mandatory** (with-thesis track). Students choose an advisor via a Consultation Preference Form (available on department site), propose a topic, and defend before a jury. Plagiarism software report required; bound and digital copies submitted after successful defense. A seminar course (typically final coursework semester) bridges coursework and research.

- **Distinctive elements:**
  - Direct connection to the **AI Institute (YZE)** on the same campus, with dedicated AI Labs, Robotics & Automation Lab, and Data Science & Analytics Lab — offering infrastructure beyond the department itself.
  - **National Technology Specialization Programmes** (Ministry of Industry & Technology), focusing on AI, Autonomous Driving, and Chip Design, run jointly with leading Turkish companies — graduate students eligible to participate.
  - Active TÜBİTAK-funded projects (e.g., AI-powered phishing simulation — TÜBİTAK 1505; Turkey's first large-scale satellite data AI platform in the energy sector).
  - Research assistants embedded as co-instructors and project contributors alongside faculty.

## 5) Admissions & Eligibility (Hard Requirements)

- **Academic (GPA):** Minimum 2.20 / 4.00 undergraduate GPA. [Source](https://ai.eng.ankara.edu.tr/en/masters-degree/)

- **Standardized test (ALES):** Minimum score of **70 in the numerical (quantitative) section**. ALES is a Turkish graduate admissions exam; international equivalents (GRE) may be accepted per Ankara University Graduate School regulations at the discretion of the institute. [Source](https://ai.eng.ankara.edu.tr/en/masters-degree/)

- **Language (foreign language exam):** Minimum **55 points** from YDS, YÖKDİL, or an ÖSYM-recognised international equivalent (e.g., TOEFL, IELTS). Valid for both Turkish and international applicants. Note: programme language is Turkish, so a Turkish language certificate (TÖMER C1 or Yunus Emre Institute) will also be required for instruction. [Source](https://ai.eng.ankara.edu.tr/en/masters-degree/), [Ankara University Graduate Regulations](https://fenbilimleri.ankara.edu.tr/yonetmelik/)

- **Background/prereqs:** Bachelor's degree from one of the following fields: Artificial Intelligence Engineering, Artificial Intelligence and Data Engineering, Computer Engineering, Software Engineering, Information Systems Engineering, Electrical & Electronics Engineering, Mechatronics Engineering, Mathematical Engineering, Control & Automation Engineering, Industrial Engineering, Mathematics & Statistics. Candidates from other backgrounds may be required to complete a Scientific Preparation Programme (max 2 semesters, not counted toward degree duration). [Source](https://ai.eng.ankara.edu.tr/en/masters-degree/)

- **Documents (standard Ankara University graduate application):**
  - Bachelor's diploma / transcript (notarized translation to Turkish if original is in another language)
  - ALES score certificate
  - Foreign language exam certificate (YDS / YÖKDİL / international equivalent)
  - Passport copy
  - For foreign graduates: YÖK (Council of Higher Education) equivalency certificate
  - Completed online application via [basvuru.ankara.edu.tr](https://basvuru.ankara.edu.tr/)
  - Consultation Preference Form (advisor preference) — download from department site
  [Reference — Graduate School Spring Application Guide](https://fenbilimleri.ankara.edu.tr/wp-content/uploads/sites/79/2025/01/24-25_Spring_Application.pdf)

- **References:** Not explicitly listed as required for this programme's published entry criteria; confirm with the Graduate School of Natural and Applied Sciences (Fen Bilimleri Enstitüsü).

- **Written/science exam:** A Bilim Sınavı (science/written exam) may be required; the department page references an information link about it. Confirm timing and format directly with department.

  - Fall intake: applications typically open in July (2025 example: July 7–18, with applicant info meeting on July 10, 2025).
  - Spring intake: applications typically in January (2025 Spring example had a deadline in late January 2025, with exams on January 29, 2025).
  - Applications are submitted via [basvuru.ankara.edu.tr](https://basvuru.ankara.edu.tr/)
  - [AI Institute 2025–2026 Fall Announcement](https://yze.ankara.edu.tr/en/2025/)

Monitor [ai.eng.ankara.edu.tr](https://ai.eng.ankara.edu.tr/en/masters-degree/) and [fenbilimleri.ankara.edu.tr](https://fenbilimleri.ankara.edu.tr/).

- **Türkiye Scholarships deadlines (for scholarship route):**
  - 2026 application window: **10 January – 20 February 2026** (this cycle has already closed).
  - For the 2027 intake cycle: next window expected January–February 2027.
  - Evaluation: March–May; Interviews: June–July; Results: August.
  - [Official Türkiye Scholarships Announcement](https://turkiyeburslari.gov.tr/announcements/turkiye-scholarships-2026-applications-121)

- **Evaluation criteria:**
  - Academic performance (GPA, ALES score)
  - Foreign language score
  - Written science exam (Bilim Sınavı) — if applicable

- **Notes:** Türkiye Scholarships include university placement as part of the scholarship — the applicant selects up to 3 university/programme preferences. The scholarship body liaises with the university. There is no separate university application if admitted via the scholarship.

## 7) Funding / Tuition / Cost

- **Tuition:** As a public Turkish state university, Ankara University charges nominal tuition fees (set annually by the Council of Ministers). International students accepted through Türkiye Scholarships pay **zero tuition** — it is fully covered by the scholarship.

- **Türkiye Scholarships coverage (Master's level):**
  - Full tuition fee waiver
  - Monthly stipend (~5,000 TL/month for MSc, subject to annual adjustment)
  - Free university dormitory accommodation
  - Round-trip flight ticket (arrival and graduation)
  - Health insurance
  - One-year free Turkish language course (TÖMER) — relevant since programme is in Turkish
  - Access to Türkiye Alumni Network
  [Official Türkiye Scholarships](https://www.turkiyeburslari.gov.tr/scholarshipsprograms)

- **Cost of living in Ankara (2026 estimates, secondary sources):**
  - University dormitory: ~$100–$300/month (government KYK dorms cheapest at ~725–1,200 TL/month)
  - Shared private apartment: ~€250–€400/month
  - Food: ~€150–250/month
  - Transportation (student card): ~300 TL/month
  - Total estimated monthly student budget: **€500–€1,000** (or ~$650–$1,050 USD), depending on lifestyle
  - Ankara is approximately 27% cheaper overall than Istanbul
  [Source — Homa Immigration, 2026](https://homaimg.com/living-costs-in-ankara/); [Skyline Education cost guide](https://skylineistedu.com/en/blog/ultimate-guide-to-student-living-costs-in-turkey)

## 10) Risks, Ambiguities, and To-Confirm Checklist

- **Programme is very new (est. July 2024):** First cohort admitted Spring 2025. No published alumni outcomes, placement statistics, or established reputation yet. Verify current cohort size and faculty supervision capacity before applying.

- **Language of instruction is Turkish:** Despite the programme being listed in the Bologna system in English, the official language field states Turkish. International students accepted via Türkiye Scholarships will attend a one-year TÖMER course first — confirm whether the MSc can begin concurrently or only after C1 certification.

- **ALES requirement for international students:** ALES is a domestic Turkish exam. While the Ankara University postgraduate regulations allow GRE equivalence (per the Graduate School), the department page only mentions ALES 70. **Confirm with the Graduate School of Natural and Applied Sciences whether GRE/GMAT is accepted as a substitute and at what threshold** — this is critical for a foreign applicant.

- **Foreign language score floor (55) is low:** A score of 55/100 on YDS/YÖKDİL corresponds to roughly IELTS 5.5–6.0. For international applicants submitting an IELTS/TOEFL, confirm the ÖSYM conversion table being used. [ÖSYM equivalency table](https://www.osym.gov.tr/) should be verified.

- **Application deadlines for Fall 2026/2027 not yet published:** Based on prior cycles, expect an announcement in June–July 2026. The department's online application portal ([basvuru.ankara.edu.tr](https://basvuru.ankara.edu.tr/)) will list the exact window.

- **Türkiye Scholarships 2026 cycle already closed (deadline was 20 February 2026).** If targeting 2026/2027, the next scholarship cycle will open approximately January 2027. Applicants must wait for that cycle.

- **References/recommendation letters:** Not listed in the published entry requirements for this MSc. However, the Türkiye Scholarships application may require a reference letter. **Confirm whether the university also requires letters separately from the scholarship application.**

- **Bilim Sınavı (written science exam):** The department page references a link "for information about the science exam." The format, topics, and whether it is required for all applicants (including international/scholarship students) must be confirmed with the department directly.

- **Quota for international students:** The AI Institute's Fall 2025–2026 intake announcement included specific quota numbers (domestic + international separately). Check the next announcement for available slots — the programme may have a very small international quota.

- **No published course catalogue in English:** The detailed course list is on the BBS system, largely in Turkish. Review the BBS "Course List and Crediting" page ([link structure](https://bbs.ankara.edu.tr/Ders_Plani.aspx?bno=4624&bot=2249)) for the complete course list and credit requirements before writing the motivation letter.
