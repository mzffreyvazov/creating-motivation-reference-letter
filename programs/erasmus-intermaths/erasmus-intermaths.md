## 1) Executive Summary (5–8 bullets)

- InterMaths is a **2-year, 120 ECTS, full-time Erasmus Mundus Joint Master** in _Mathematical Modelling and Simulation_ with applications to biomedical sciences and industrial engineering, funded by the European Commission under Grant Agreement no. 101240735 (2025–2029 cycle).
- Students study at **at least two universities in at least two different countries**, following a structured mobility path: Semester 1 in L'Aquila (Italy, mandatory for all), Semester 2 in Hamburg or Vienna, Year 2 in one of six specialization tracks spread across the five partner universities.
- **Six specialization tracks** are available for Year 2: Computational Fluid Dynamics in Industry (Vienna), Computational Methods in Imaging (Hamburg), Decision Making and Logistics (Barcelona), Mathematical Modelling for Healthcare (L'Aquila), Modelling and Simulation for Natural Disasters (L'Aquila), and **Stochastics for Biological and Artificial Neural Networks (Nice)** — the last being the most relevant for AI/neuroscience-oriented applicants.
- The programme awards a **Joint Master Degree** signed by all institutions attended (UAQ + UAB by default, plus any others where at least one semester was spent).
- **20 full EMJM scholarships** are available for the 2026 cohort, providing a monthly allowance of **€1,400/month** for the full 2 years (≈€33,600 total). Scholarship deadline: **February 25, 2026 at 1 PM CET** — _4 days from today (February 21, 2026). Urgent action required._
- Tuition is waived for EMJM scholarship holders; non-scholarship consortium holders pay a reduced participation cost of **€3,000 total** (€750/semester × 4).
- Assessment is 90 ECTS by taught courses + 30 ECTS by Master's thesis (often co-developed with industry partners), totalling 120 ECTS over two years.
- Career destinations include research (PhD programs), pharmaceutical/biomedical industry, manufacturing, software, finance, and AI/data-driven enterprises through industrial partner connections.

## 2) What the Program Optimizes For (Selection Signals)

- **Stated values:** Interdisciplinarity, mathematical rigour applied to real-world societal challenges, innovation, European mobility, industry–academia collaboration, teamwork and leadership during thesis, societal impact (medicine, environment, industry).
- **Ideal candidate signals:** Strong mathematics background from any of Mathematics, Applied Mathematics, Physics, Engineering, or Computer Science; ability to translate challenging real-world problems into mathematical models; interest in working in international, multidisciplinary teams; prior quantitative coursework depth matters more than the degree label.
- **Keyword emphasis throughout official materials:** "interdisciplinary", "mathematical modelling", "scientific computing", "real-world problems", "professional scientist", "research potential", "industry collaboration", "biomedical sciences", "industrial engineering", "societal challenges."
- **Comparative selection:** The committee ranks all applicants after the deadline; no rolling admission. Track preferences are factored in but the committee balances cohort distribution across tracks. Students can flag "non-preferable" tracks and are guaranteed not to be assigned there.
- **Evidence (links):** [Profile & Objectives](https://www.intermaths.eu/erasmus-mundus/about#profile), [Selection Process](https://www.intermaths.eu/erasmus-mundus/apply#selection)

## 3) Curriculum & Academic Experience

- **Structure:** 2 years / 4 semesters / 120 ECTS total. 90 ECTS from courses (3 taught semesters) + 30 ECTS from Master's thesis (Semester 4). Medium of instruction: English throughout all five institutions.
    
- **Semester 1 — L'Aquila: Foundations of Applied Mathematics (all students)**
    
    - Functional Analysis & Abstract Analysis (9 ECTS) — Banach/Hilbert spaces, spectral theory, measure theory
    - Partial Differential Equations (6 ECTS) — heat, Laplace, wave equations; characteristics; Green's functions
    - Dynamical Systems & Bifurcation Theory (6 ECTS) — nonlinear dynamics, stability, center manifold theory
    - Continuum Mechanics
    - Control Systems
    - Italian language course (A1 level, compulsory pass)
- **Semester 2 — Hamburg or Vienna: Numerical–Modelling Training**
    
    - _Vienna track:_ Finite Element Methods (FEM), Numerical ODEs/PDEs, Numerical Optimization, Parallel Computing, Variational Calculus (profs. Praetorius, Schoeberl, Arnold)
    - _Hamburg track:_ Scientific computing, probability theory, numerical methods, parallelisation (led by Taraz, Lindner, LeBorne, Ruprecht)
    - German language course (A1 level, compulsory pass)
- **Year 2 — Six Specialization Tracks (course-level detail):**

#### Track 1 — Stochastics for Biological and Artificial Neural Networks | Nice (UniCA)

_Semester 2 routing: Hamburg (TUHH)_  
**Best for:** AI/neuroscience; stochastic foundations of deep learning; computational neuroscience.

**Key faculty:** Prof. François Delarue (AMS Doob Prize 2020, mean-field games), Prof. Patricia Reynaud-Bouret (Director, NeuroMod Institute, spike train statistics), Prof. Cédric Bernardin, Dr. Etienne Tanré (Human Brain Project member), Prof. Alice Guyon (CNRS, neuropharmacology).

|Course|ECTS|Core content|
|---|---|---|
|Stochastic Calculus with Applications to Neuroscience|6|Brownian motion, Itô SDEs, diffusion processes, Markov property, martingales; applications to integrate-and-fire neural models and ion channel stochasticity|
|Stochastics for Machine Learning|6|SGD theory, Langevin dynamics, Hamilton-Jacobi equations, Nash equilibria, mean-field limits of overparameterised networks, GANs, deep networks, Python practicals|
|Stochastic Models in Neurocognition & Statistical Inference|6|Point processes for spike trains, statistical inference from neural data, neurocognitive modelling (Reynaud-Bouret)|
|Behavioural and Cognitive Neuroscience|6|Neurophysiology, neuroanatomy, cognition, molecular pharmacology of neurons (Alice Guyon, CNRS)|
|Monte Carlo & Quasi-Monte Carlo Methods|6|Simulation of stochastic processes, variance reduction, applied probabilistic computing|

**Thesis opportunities:** NeuroMod Institute, CNRS J.A. Dieudonné lab, **Fotonower** (AI/image processing company — industrial partner).  
**Unique to this track:** Only track in the programme fusing rigorous stochastics, biological neural networks, AND artificial neural networks in one curriculum. Direct connection to the Human Brain Project.

#### Track 2 — Computational Methods in Imaging | Hamburg (TUHH)

_Semester 2 routing: Hamburg (TUHH)_  
**Best for:** Medical imaging, AI in clinical settings, inverse problems, mathematical regularisation, applied ML in diagnostics.

**Key faculty:** Prof. Tobias Knopp (TUHH + UKE dual appointment, mathematical modelling in medicine), Prof. Armin Iske (UHH, CT mathematics and approximation theory), Prof. Martin Burger (DESY, image reconstruction), Dr. Jens-Peter M. For the 2025–2029 cycle, TUHH refocused from general biomedical applications to "Computational Methods for Imaging" specifically — covering imaging from mathematical, physical, and computer science perspectives, with AI methods explicitly integrated. Four compulsory Semester 3 modules are now dedicated to imaging alone. A new UHH module was added for this funding period. This curriculum did not exist in its current form before 2026.

|Course|ECTS|Core content|
|---|---|---|
|Mathematical Image Processing & Reconstruction|6|Image formation and reconstruction; forward problem modelling; classical regularisation (Tikhonov, TV); **modern deep-learning inverse problems**; Bayesian uncertainty quantification (inverse problems view)|
|Tomographic Imaging & Reconstruction|6|CT/MRI physics; Radon transform; back projection; Fourier slice theorem; algebraic reconstruction techniques; compressed sensing; magnetic particle imaging — reconstruction algorithms from imaging operators (Knopp/Iske)|
|Medical Image Analysis|6|Diffusion equations for image processing; image segmentation and registration; inverse problems in clinical context; functional analysis foundations|

**Research ecosystem:** TUHH + UHH + UKE run joint projects at FMTHH in biomobility, networked implants, and imaging. Prof. Knopp's UKE lab is embedded in the medical centre — students gain rare exposure to live clinical data environments.

## 5) Admissions & Eligibility (Hard Requirements)

- **Academic:** Bachelor's degree (at least 180 ECTS or equivalent) in Mathematics, Applied Mathematics, Physics, Engineering, Computer Science, or similar field with a **very strong mathematical background**. Students in their final year may apply if they will graduate before the program starts (typically by August of intake year). The degree title alone is insufficient — the committee reviews transcripts and course descriptions. [Requirements](https://www.intermaths.eu/erasmus-mundus/apply#requirements)
    
- **Background/prereqs:** A specific template must be completed listing all mathematics courses taken in the Bachelor's; this is a required upload. Strong prior coursework in analysis, differential equations, and computing is implicitly expected. No explicit minimum GPA stated publicly, but selection is highly competitive. [Apply Page](https://www.intermaths.eu/erasmus-mundus/apply)
    
- **Language:** English at CEFR B2 level minimum, certified by one of:
    
    - IELTS Academic: overall band ≥ 6.0
    - TOEFL iBT: overall ≥ 80
    - PTE Academic: overall ≥ 56
    - Cambridge C1 Advanced: overall score ≥ 169
    - Waiver: if undergraduate or secondary education was in an English-medium institution for ≥1 year, a letter from the institution replaces the test. [Language Requirements](https://www.intermaths.eu/erasmus-mundus/apply#requirements)
- **Documents required:**
    
    - Completed online application form (via PICA/Cineca platform at pica.cineca.it/univaq/2026-network-intermaths)
    - CV/Résumé (Europass format preferred)
    - Photo (ID-size, plain background)
    - Passport / valid ID
    - Transcripts of all university studies
    - Template listing all Bachelor's mathematics courses
    - English language certificate (or waiver documentation)
    - Motivation/Letter of Intent (max 1 page)
    - 2 academic reference letters (invited via the application tool's reference system; can be submitted after the main deadline)
    - Signed PDF receipt ("Sign and Submit" step — mandatory to complete submission) [Documents Checklist](https://www.intermaths.eu/erasmus-mundus/apply)
- **References:** 2 academic referees; invited through the application system; reference letters may be submitted after the main scholarship deadline (flexibility on exact timing). [Apply Page](https://www.intermaths.eu/erasmus-mundus/apply)
    
- **Scholarship eligibility note:** Prior EMJM scholarship holders are ineligible for another EMJM scholarship but may apply as non-scholarship students. [Scholarship Page](https://www.intermaths.eu/erasmus-mundus/apply#emjm-scholarships)

## 6) Application Process & Deadlines

- **Scholarship/main deadline:** **February 25, 2026 at 1:00 PM CET** (for EMJM scholarship consideration and priority evaluation). ⚠️ _As of today (February 21, 2026), this deadline is in 4 days. Urgent action required if not yet submitted._
- **Later deadline:** Applications submitted after February 25 may still be considered for admission (non-scholarship), but the exact late deadline is not publicly specified. Contact the consortium directly.
- **Reference letters:** Can be submitted after the main application deadline; the applicant must send referees an invitation via the application tool's reference system.
- **Selection results:** Published approximately one month after the deadline, on the program website. No rolling admissions — all applications evaluated together after the deadline.
- **Application platform:** PICA/Cineca at [pica.cineca.it/univaq/2026-network-intermaths](https://pica.cineca.it/univaq/2026-network-intermaths)
- **Sign & Submit step:** Critical — applicants must generate a PDF receipt, sign it (handwritten signature recommended), and upload it back. Application is not considered submitted until this step is complete.
- **Single application covers three programs:** The same form covers InterMaths (EMJM), MathMods (European Joint MSc), and RealMaths (International MSc); applicant selects preference level for each.
- **Evaluation criteria:** Comparative ranking based on academic achievement, mathematical background (course content and grades), motivation letter, CV, and references. No interview is mentioned.
- **Mobility assignment:** Track preference is collected in the application; students guaranteed not to receive tracks marked "non-preferable." Final mobility assignment communicated at the start of Semester 1.
- **Enrollment deposit (non-scholarship):** ~€344 non-refundable deposit within 30 days of selection notification; remaining fees cleared before Semester 1.
- **Evidence:** [Apply Page](https://www.intermaths.eu/erasmus-mundus/apply), [Deadlines](https://www.intermaths.eu/erasmus-mundus/apply#deadlines)
