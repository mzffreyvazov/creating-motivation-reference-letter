## 1) Executive Summary (5–8 bullets)

- MathMods is a **2-year, 120 ECTS, full-time Joint MSc** in *Mathematical Modelling in Engineering*, run by 5 European universities. It has been running continuously since 2008, making it the oldest and most alumni-rich programme in the InterMaths Network.
- The degree focus is explicitly **engineering-oriented**: applying rigorous mathematical tools (PDEs, numerics, optimization, stochastics) to solve real-world engineering, industrial, financial, and biological problems — contrasting with InterMaths' stronger biomedical/interdisciplinary framing.
- **Mobility structure:** Semester 1 at L'Aquila (Theory, common to all), Semester 2 at Hamburg (Numerics, common to all), Semester 3 + 4 at one of 5 Year-2 partners across 6 specialization tracks.
- The consortium partners are: **UAQ** (L'Aquila, Italy), **UHH** (University of Hamburg, Germany), **UniCA** (Nice, France), **LUH** (Leibniz University Hannover, Germany), **UGR** (University of Granada, Spain). Note: different partner set from InterMaths — Hamburg here is the *University of Hamburg* (UHH), not Hamburg University of Technology (TUHH).
- **Tuition with partial waiver:** Only **€2,000 total** (€500/semester × 4) for the 2025 cohort — one of the most affordable international joint MSc programmes in Europe. The 2026 figure is slightly different: **€3,000 total** (€750/semester × 4) based on the current portal information.
- **No EMJM scholarship**, but students are eligible for UAQ Travel & Installation grant (~€3,000), Erasmus+ Mobility grant (~€300/month for up to 12 months), ADSU regional grants, and a Pre-Master Bonus. Combined: up to €550/month for 12 months.
- **Results notification:** April 30, 2026. Reference letters accepted until March 11, 2026.
- Career outcomes include R&D roles, PhD entry, finance and insurance (quant modelling), industrial engineering, and data science; graduates are described by the programme as occupying "the type of positions they sought" in all known cases.

- **Ideal candidate signals:** Strong mathematical background from any of Mathematics, Applied Mathematics, Physics, or Engineering; capable of abstract modelling AND numerical computation; adaptable across application domains (from fluid dynamics to finance to biomathematics); prior experience with problem-solving and project work preferred.

## 3) Curriculum & Academic Experience

- **Structure:** 2 years / 4 semesters / 120 ECTS total. Each semester awards 30 ECTS. Semesters 1 and 2 are common to all students. Semesters 3 and 4 are at one Year-2 partner institution (track-specific).

### Semester 1 — L'Aquila (UAQ): Theory — September to March

Identical institution and location as InterMaths Semester 1, but slightly different course framing (more engineering-facing):

- **Applied Partial Differential Equations** (6 ECTS) — heat, wave, Laplace equations; characteristics; Green's functions; Fourier methods; similarity solutions
- **Functional Analysis** (6 ECTS) — Banach and Hilbert spaces; distributions; Sobolev spaces; operator theory; essential for all advanced numerics
- **Dynamical Systems & Bifurcation Theory** (6 ECTS) — nonlinear ODEs, stability analysis, bifurcations, phase-plane methods
- **Continuum Mechanics** — modelling physical systems as continuous media
- **Italian Language** (A1 level, compulsory pass, credits awarded)
- **3-week Preparatory Course** (pre-semester) — linear algebra, complex variables, ODE basics, probability — ensures common baseline across all students

### Semester 2 — University of Hamburg (UHH): Numerics — April to July

Note: this is the **University of Hamburg** (UHH), *not* Hamburg University of Technology (TUHH). MathMods uses UHH; InterMaths uses TUHH. Same city, different institutions.

- **Numerical Approximation of PDEs by Finite Differences & Finite Volumes** (6 ECTS) — consistency, stability, convergence, numerical diffusion/dispersion; FreeEEM software
- **Finite Element Methods** (6 ECTS) — FE spaces, linear algebra systems, error analysis in Sobolev spaces, a posteriori error analysis
- **Nonlinear Optimization** (6 ECTS) — minimization of nonlinear functionals, constrained/unconstrained algorithms, convex optimization, nonsmooth optimization
- **German Language** (A1 level, compulsory pass, credits awarded)
- Optional engineering-oriented modules (computer science / industrial case studies)

### Semester 3 — Six Specialization Tracks at Year-2 Partners

| Track | Location | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Mathematical Models in Social Sciences | L'Aquila (UAQ) | Traffic flow, fluid dynamics, social dynamics, agent models, conservation laws |
| **Mathematical Modelling & Optimisation** | **L'Aquila (UAQ)** | **Data analytics, stochastic processes, combinatorial optimization, wavelets, signal processing** |
| Modelling & Simulation of Complex Systems | Hamburg (UHH) | Complex systems, engineering applications, synergy with TUHH |
| **Mathematical Modelling Applications to Finance** | **Nice (UniCA)** | **Stochastic calculus, probabilistic numerical methods, advanced stochastics, statistical methods for financial markets** |
| Mathematical Models of Nonlinear Phenomena | Hannover (LUH) | Nonlinear wave equations, solitons, dispersive PDEs, mathematical physics |
| **Mathematical Modelling. Biomathematics** | **Granada (UGR)** | **Collective behaviour, emergent phenomena, chemotaxis, game theory in biology, ML & AI (new), numerical analysis of PDEs** |

### Track Deep-Dives (Most Relevant for AI/ML/Neuroscience)

**Granada (UGR) — Biomathematics Track** *(most distinctive for AI-interested applicants)*
- Numerical Analysis of PDEs & Approximation (6 ECTS) — FDM, FEM, spectral methods
- **Fundamentals of Machine Learning & Computational Optimal Transport** (6 ECTS, **NEW**) — deep learning theory, large-scale/distributed optimization, causal inference, fairness and safety in AI, Python-based practicals; optimal transport theory
- Minicourses & Guest Seminars series: complex systems and emergent behaviour in biomedicine, nonlinear modelling of group interactions, pattern formation, cellular communication, game theory in biology, chemotaxis, quorum sensing, immune system modelling
- Applied focus: connecting the mathematical foundations learned in Year 1 to living systems

**Nice (UniCA) — Mathematical Finance Track**
- Stochastic Calculus & Applications to Mathematical Finance (6 ECTS, mandatory) — continuous-time stochastic processes, physics/finance/biology applications
- Probabilistic Numerical Methods (6 ECTS, mandatory)
- Advanced Stochastics & Applications to Mathematical Finance (6 ECTS, mandatory)
- Optional: statistical methods (3 courses), numerical methods

**L'Aquila (UAQ) — Mathematical Modelling & Optimisation Track**
- Advanced Methods in Applied Mathematics — Sobolev spaces, nonlinear conservation laws, semigroup theory
- Combinatorial Optimization — matroids, matching, TSP approximation, branch-and-bound
- Wavelets & Frames — Fourier analysis, Daubechies wavelets, signal processing, optimization in approximation theory
- Stochastic Processes & Forecasting
- Dynamic Multi-agent Network Analysis

### Semester 4 — Thesis

- Held at UHH, UAQ, or UniCA (depending on Year-2 track)
- 30 ECTS; oral examination + formal thesis defense
- Topic can be from industry partner or academic supervisor
- Possibility of internship at collaborating company during thesis semester
- Full list of private industry/enterprise partners available: [Thesis Industries](https://www.mathmods.eu/structure/semester4/item/740-list-of-private-industries-enterprises-for-internships)

- **Degree awarded:** Joint or Double MSc Degree depending on Year-2 destination. All five partners can appear on the diploma.

- **Evidence (links):** [Semester 1](https://www.mathmods.eu/structure/semester1), [Semester 2](https://www.mathmods.eu/structure/semester2/semester2-uhh), [Semester 3](https://www.mathmods.eu/structure/semester3), [Granada Track](https://www.mathmods.eu/structure/semester3/ugr), [Nice Track](http://www.mathmods.eu/structure/semester3/uns), [UAQ Optimisation Track](https://www.mathmods.eu/structure/semester3/uaq-optimisation)

## 10) Risks, Ambiguities, and To-Confirm Checklist

- ⚠️ **No EMJM scholarship available.** This is the biggest financial disadvantage vs. InterMaths. The maximum realistic monthly support from all grants combined (~€550/month) is significantly less than the InterMaths EMJM scholarship (€1,400/month). If funding is a priority, InterMaths must be ranked first.

- ⚠️ **Tuition discrepancy:** The 2025 cohort page shows €2,000 total with waiver (€500/semester), while the 2026 figure in the portal appears to be €3,000 total (€750/semester). This aligns with the InterMaths 2026 fee structure. **Confirm directly with the consortium** which figure applies to the 2026 cohort — there may be a page that hasn't been updated yet.

- ❓ **Year-2 track assignment not guaranteed:** Same constraint as InterMaths — the Scientific Committee assigns tracks for cohort balance. Students can express preferences and flag non-preferred tracks, but assignment is not guaranteed. Flag preferred tracks clearly in the application.

- ❓ **Semester 2 at UHH (University of Hamburg) vs. TUHH (TU Hamburg):** These are two different universities sharing the same city. MathMods uses UHH; InterMaths uses TUHH. Students doing both applications via the same form will study in slightly different Hamburg environments, with different faculties. Important to understand which you're going to.

- ❓ **"Life after MathMods" survey is from 2017** — the only publicly available employment outcome data is nearly a decade old and reflects cohorts 2008–2015. Current outcomes are anecdotally described as positive but there are no recent published statistics.

- ❓ **Thesis location options are limited:** Thesis (Semester 4) is held at UHH, UAQ, or UniCA only — fewer options than Semester 3. If a specific institution for thesis is important, confirm this matches the Year-2 track.

- ❓ **ADSU grant income criteria:** The Abruzzo regional grant (up to €3,000/year + accommodation + free meals) requires low-income status under Italian criteria. For international students, the income calculation method should be confirmed with UAQ International Relations Office.

- ❓ **MAECI grant incompatibility:** The Italian Ministry of Foreign Affairs grant may be incompatible with ADSU and other regional grants. Read terms carefully before accepting any combination of grants.

- ❓ **Motivation letter addressed differently:** For MathMods, the letter should be addressed to the "MathMods Evaluation Committee." For InterMaths, no specific addressee is named. Since both are read by the same overall network committee, this is largely stylistic — but follow the convention exactly.
