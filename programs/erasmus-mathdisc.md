## 1) Executive Summary (5–8 bullets)

- MATHS-DISC is a 2-year, 120 ECTS Erasmus Mundus Joint Master in **Mathematical Modelling, Simulation, and Data Science for Industrial and Societal Challenges**, launched for the first time with intake September/October 2025.
- The programme trains graduates in a combined MSODE skill set — **Modelling, Simulation, Optimisation in Data-rich Environments** — integrating three key enabling technologies to minimise re-skilling across professional careers.
- Students study at **two or three of the five consortium universities** across Europe, ensuring genuine multi-country mobility.
- There are **8 specialisation tracks** (CICO, HPCQF, SML, FIN, MED, LOGT, AGR, NRG), each focused on a strategic domain for the digital and green transition.
- **Up to 15 EMJM + 4 NDICI scholarships per year** (€1,400/month for 24 months, covering tuition, travel, accommodation, and insurance) are available for top-ranked international applicants.
- The **semester 4 Internship & Master Thesis** takes place within an industry or research partner (Siemens, Bosch, Intel, Bayer, d-fine, Otto Bock, NeuronsLab, etc.).
- **Application deadline for 2026 intake (Round 1): 2 March 2026.** A second round opens in May–June.
- Award is a **multiple degree** certificate issued by the consortium universities, accompanied by a Diploma Supplement documenting the full mobility path.

## 3) Curriculum & Academic Experience

- **Structure:** 2 years / 4 semesters / 120 ECTS. Semester 1 at any consortium university (assigned by Study Plan Committee). Semester 2 at UNIVR (modelling & optimisation focus) or BUW (computing & simulation focus). Semesters 3–4 at the specialisation university, ending with an industry internship and Master Thesis.
    
- **Semester 1 — Foundations/Background (30 ECTS):** Core topics across all host universities include Mathematical Modelling, Functional Analysis / Numerical Methods, Partial Differential Equations, Computer Programming & Simulation, Data Mining, and Probability/Statistics. Courses vary slightly by host but cover the same FO/MO/CS areas.
    
- **Semester 2 — Methods (30 ECTS):**
    
    - _UNIVR profile (MO emphasis):_ Optimization, Stochastic Calculus, Numerical Modelling and Optimization, Statistical Learning, electives in Analytical Mechanics or Sustainable Entrepreneurship.
    - _BUW profile (CS emphasis):_ Computer Simulation 2, Bayesian Learning, Numerical Methods 2a/2b, electives in Computational Finance, Computational Electromagnetics, Atmospheric Physics, Molecular & Materials Modelling.
- **Semesters 3–4 — Specialisation + Internship/Thesis (60 ECTS):** Dedicated to the chosen track at the selected university, culminating in an industry-based internship and Master Thesis project applying skills to real industrial or societal problems.
    
- **Distinctive elements:**
    
    - Weekly joint MSODE seminar delivered by invited scholars via the MATHS-DISC e-learning platform (active from Semester 1).
    - ECMI (European Consortium for Mathematics in Industry) Modelling Week participation.
    - Industry internship funded by the host partner.
    - Personalised study plan committee guiding each student's mobility path.
- **Evidence:** [Study Programme Detail](https://www.mathsdisc.eu/study-programme/), [Diploma Certificates](https://www.mathsdisc.eu/study-programme/diploma-certificates/)

## 6) Admissions & Eligibility (Hard Requirements)

- **Academic:** Bachelor's degree (≥ 180 ECTS or equivalent; minimum 3 years) in Mathematics, Applied Mathematics, Physics, Engineering, Computer Science, Economics, or Statistics — with **strong mathematical background**. CGPA must be **≥ 75%**. BSc must be completed before **31 July** (non-EU) or **30 September** (EU) of the intake year. [Link](https://www.mathsdisc.eu/admission/how-to-apply/)
- **Background/prereqs:** Calculus (18 ECTS), Linear Algebra (6 ECTS), Numerical Analysis (6 ECTS), Programming (6 ECTS), Statistics (6 ECTS). [Link](https://www.mathsdisc.eu/admission/how-to-apply/)
- **Language:** English fluency required. Accepted tests: IELTS ≥ 6.5 | TOEFL IBT ≥ 80 / CBT 220 / ITP 550 | Cambridge B2 First ≥ 173 | Cambridge C1 Advanced ≥ 160 | TOEIC ≥ 800. Results must be no older than 3 years. **Applicants who studied entirely in English may be exempt with an official letter from their university.** [Link](https://www.mathsdisc.eu/admission/how-to-apply/)
- **Documents required (PDF, in English):**
    - National ID or Passport
    - Official academic transcripts (ISCED Level 6 mandatory) with certified English translations
    - CGPA score documentation
    - Proof of English proficiency
    - CV in Europass format
    - Motivation letter
    - Indication of scholarship type requested (EMJM / NDICI / Consortium / Self-funded) [Link](https://www.mathsdisc.eu/admission/how-to-apply/)
- **References:** Not explicitly listed as required documents on the official page — **confirm directly with the consortium** (see Section 10).

## 7) Application Process & Deadlines

- **Round 1 Deadline (2026 intake):** Applications open December 15, 2025 → **Close 2 March 2026**. Covers EMJM scholarships, NDICI scholarships, consortium-funded scholarships, and self-funded students. [Link](https://www.mathsdisc.eu/)
    
- **Round 2 Deadline:** Opens May–June (exact date not yet published for 2026). Self-funded students and reserve list candidates may apply. [Link](https://www.mathsdisc.eu/admission/how-to-apply/)
    
- **Platform:** Applications submitted via [UniCandidate portal](https://www.unicandidate.com/mathematical-modelling-simulation-and-data-science-for-industrial-and-societal-challenges-2026-2028).
    
- **Selection timeline:** Evaluation takes place March–April (Round 1) and July (Round 2). [Link](https://www.mathsdisc.eu/admission/selection-procedure/)
    
- **Evaluation rubric (scored out of 100 + up to 30 interview points):**
    
    |Criterion|Max Score|
    |---|---|
    |Academic performance at Bachelor level|35|
    |Relevance of BSc to MATHS-DISC|25|
    |Quality of home institution|15|
    |CV aspects (work exp., qualifications, languages)|10|
    |English level|5|
    |Motivation letter|10|
    |**Pre-interview total**|**100**|
    
- **Interview:** Applicants scoring ≥ 70/100 are invited to interview. The interview assesses mathematical knowledge and motivation (scored 0–30). Only candidates with interview score ≥ 20 are admitted. **Minimum total to be admitted: 90 points (70 + 20).** [Link](https://www.mathsdisc.eu/admission/selection-procedure/)
    
- **Gender balance:** At least 30% of main list places guaranteed for each gender. [Link](https://www.mathsdisc.eu/admission/selection-procedure/)

## 8) Funding / Tuition / Cost

- **Tuition:** Scholarship holders pay **no tuition fees**. Self-funded students' fee amounts are not listed on the official page — confirm directly.
- **EMJM Scholarships (15 per year):** €1,400/month for 24 months (full programme). Covers accommodation, travel, and subsistence. Full insurance included. Awarded to highest-ranked applicants. [Link](https://www.mathsdisc.eu/study-programme/scholarships-maths-master-degree/)
- **NDICI Targeted Regions Scholarships (4 per year):** Same amount and coverage as EMJM, reserved for applicants from designated targeted regions. **Azerbaijan is classified as a targeted NDICI region under Erasmus+ — confirm eligibility at application stage.** [Link](https://www.mathsdisc.eu/study-programme/scholarships-maths-master-degree/)
- **Additional funding sources by university:**
    - **UNIVR:** ESU regional scholarships (up to €600/month); MAECI Italian Ministry grants; INdAM grants; accommodation support.
    - **BUW:** BAföG abroad; Franco-German University funding (with UGA); International Centre support.
    - **UGA:** CROUS centralised student support (meals, accommodation, transport).
    - **ULIS:** SAS scholarships; Gulbenkian New Talents scholarships; direct/indirect support.
    - **ISKPI:** Sikorsky Challenge innovation competition grants.
    - **All:** Erasmus+ exchange scholarships for additional mobility legs; industry-funded internships in Semester 4.
- **Cost-of-living guidance:** No consolidated official estimate is published. Typical student costs in Verona, Wuppertal, Grenoble, and Lisbon range €800–€1,300/month (accommodation included); Kyiv is lower. The scholarship of €1,400/month should be broadly sufficient in most host cities.
- **Evidence:** [Scholarships Page](https://www.mathsdisc.eu/study-programme/scholarships-maths-master-degree/)
