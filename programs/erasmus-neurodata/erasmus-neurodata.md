## 1) Executive Summary (5–8 bullets)

- **Pioneering interdisciplinary program** that uniquely combines neuroscience and data science to train multidisciplinary researchers capable of tackling complex problems involving the human brain in health and disease.
- **Structured mobility pathway**: Year 1 at Bar-Ilan University (Israel) for foundational training, mandatory Summer School at University of Zagreb (Croatia), Year 2 at one of four specialization tracks across partner universities, culminating in research thesis.
- **Six leading institutions** collaborate: Bar-Ilan University (Israel, coordinator), Instituto Superior Técnico/University of Lisbon (Portugal), University of Jyväskylä (Finland), University of Padua (Italy), Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam (Netherlands), University of Zagreb (Croatia).
- **Double master's degree** awarded by two partner universities depending on track selection; graduates receive internationally recognized joint credentials.
- **Generous Erasmus Mundus scholarship**: €1,400/month for 24 months (€33,600 total) covering tuition, travel, and living expenses; 10-15 scholarships per cohort; highly selective (top 10% of applicants preferred).
- **Growing demand**: Program addresses fast-rising need for brain-data expertise in neurotechnology, healthcare, AI/ML, research, and education sectors — neurotech market grew from $8.4B (2018) to $13.3B (2022).
- **Research-intensive**: First-year research internship at Gonda Multidisciplinary Brain Research Center (30+ labs), second-year thesis in specialized labs or with industry partners, exposure to cutting-edge neuroimaging, BCIs, computational modeling.
- **Application deadline**: February 5, 2026 (23:59 CET); interviews in early March 2026; results published April 2026; program starts August 2026.

## 3) Curriculum & Academic Experience

### Structure:
- **Duration**: 2 years (120 ECTS)
- **Year 1 (60 ECTS)**: Bar-Ilan University, Israel — Core foundational courses in neuroscience and data science, tailored to students' undergraduate backgrounds to bring all to same level
- **Summer School (mandatory)**: 2 weeks at University of Zagreb, Croatia — Intensive program combining theoretical principles with hands-on learning, experimental design with animal models, microcontrollers, hardware/software skills
- **Year 2 (60 ECTS)**: One of four specialization tracks at partner universities — Advanced coursework + research thesis

### Core modules (representative — Year 1 at Bar-Ilan):
- Core courses in **brain science** (neuroscience fundamentals, neural systems, cognitive neuroscience)
- Core courses in **data science** (machine learning, AI, computational modeling, data analysis)
- **Computational neuroscience** (neural circuits, information processing, mathematical modeling)
- **Placement exams** in mathematics, Python, neuroscience (prerequisite courses assigned if not passed)
- **Research internship** (optional, non-ECTS): First-year project in Gonda Brain Center labs, presented at Annual Gonda Conference (March) and Summer School

### Electives (representative — Year 2, track-dependent):
Students select electives within their chosen second-year track during Period 1 (P1) and Period 2 (P2).

### Thesis/Capstone:
- **Master's thesis** (30 ECTS typical for master programs): Conducted during Year 2 at specialization university or associated research institute/industry partner
- Preliminary results from first-year internship presented at Summer School
- Thesis defends original research contributing to brain-data science field

### Practical components:
- **First-year research internship**: Integration into Gonda Brain Research Center (30+ labs), exposure to electrophysiology, neuroimaging, molecular techniques, computational methods
- **Summer School hands-on training**: Behavioral experiments in animal models, micro-controller programming, hardware-software design
- **Industry mentoring**: Guest lectures from leading neuroscientists and data scientists, mentoring sessions with industry representatives during Summer School for networking
- **Lab rotations**: Exposure to multiple research groups across partner institutions

### Distinctive elements:
- **Placement exam system**: Ensures all students reach common baseline despite diverse backgrounds
- **Mandatory international mobility**: Erasmus Mundus requires study in 2+ countries (scholarship holders in countries other than residence)
- **Double degree**: Joint MSc awarded by two partner universities based on track
- **Gonda Brain Research Center access**: Year 1 students personally hosted by various research groups in 20-year-old center with unique Brain Sciences curriculum
- **Four specialized tracks** in Year 2 (see Section 4)

## 4) Research Ecosystem (Labs/Groups/Faculty)

### Year 1 — Bar-Ilan University (Gonda Multidisciplinary Brain Research Center)

- **Gonda Multidisciplinary Brain Research Center** — Over 30 laboratories investigating brain complexity at multiple levels, from single neurons through neural circuits to cognition and behavior. Research approaches include brain stimulation, neuroimaging (fMRI, EEG), electrophysiology, molecular techniques, computational methods, mathematical modeling, and behavioral paradigms. [Link](https://gondabrain.biu.ac.il/en)

- **Human Brain Dynamics Lab (Prof. Elana Zion Golumbic)** — Investigates how the human brain processes dynamic sensory information, attention mechanisms, and neural oscillations. Relevant for students interested in cognitive neuroscience and EEG/MEG methods. [Link](https://www.golumbiclab.org/contact)

- **Visual Perception Lab (Dr. Sharon Gilaie-Dotan)** — Investigates how human visual system function and organization enable perception and interaction with the dynamic world. Relevant for neuroimaging, cognitive neuroscience, and computational modeling of perception. [Link](https://gondabrain.biu.ac.il/en)

- **Prof. Alon Korngreen (Center Director)** — Head of Gonda Center since 2019, expertise in neural circuits, information processing, and electrophysiology. Provides leadership and mentorship for NeuroData program. [Link](https://gondabrain.biu.ac.il/en/node/1)

- **Prof. Rafi Haddad's Lab** — Research on neural synchronization and information transmission in the brain, including how thousands of neurons coordinate activity. Example research: found synchronized neurons transmit information more powerfully than asynchronous neurons. [Link](https://studyinternational.com/news/bar-ilan-university-where-future-experts-in-the-human-mind-and-brain-are-made/)

### Year 2 Specialization Tracks

- **Track 1: Biomedical Engineering — Instituto Superior Técnico, University of Lisbon (Portugal)** — Focuses on biomedical and data science engineering aspects of neuroscience, applications for brain research and neurotechnologies, computational modeling in neuroscience. [Link](https://www.neurodata-master.org/programme)

- **Track 2: Cognitive Neuroscience and Clinical Neuropsychology with Data Processing — University of Padua (Italy)** — Designed for psychology/cognitive neuroscience students, focuses on basic and clinical aspects of cognitive neurosciences, data science approaches to neurofunctional correlates of human mind, applications in diagnosis, rehabilitation, and cognitive enhancement. [Link](https://www.neurodata-master.org/programme)

- **Track 3: Systems Neuroscience and Data Processing — Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam (Netherlands)** — Focus on fundamental, behavioral, and clinical neuroscience research, system neuroscience expertise, connections with Amsterdam UMC and CNCR (Centre for Neurogenomics and Cognitive Research). [Link](https://www.neurodata-master.org/programme)

- **Track 4: Cognitive Neuroscience and Neuroimaging with Data Processing — University of Jyväskylä (Finland)** — Specializes in neuroimaging and data processing in developmental, neurolinguistic, and cognitive neuroscience. Advanced knowledge in neuroimaging techniques and data science-oriented analysis/visualization. Long history in cognitive/behavioral neuroscience and computational sciences. [Link](https://www.neurodata-master.org/programme)

## 6) Application Process & Deadlines

### Deadlines:
- **Application opens**: November 15, 2025 (12:00 CET)
- **Application deadline**: February 5, 2026 (23:59 CET) — HARD DEADLINE
- **Any application submitted after February 5, 2026 will be automatically rejected**
- **Interviews**: Early March 2026 (for shortlisted candidates)
- **Final results**: April 2026
- **Visa procedure deadline**: Before August 2026
- **Program starts**: August 2026
[Link](https://www.neurodata-master.org/online-application)

### Evaluation:
**Two-round selection process:**

**Round 1 — Eligibility and Pre-Nomination:**
- Selection committee reviews application files via common portal
- Evaluation criteria:
  1. **Academic ranking** (GPA, class standing)
  2. **Relevance of bachelor's degree** (to neuroscience/data science)
  3. **Reference quality** (strength of recommendation letters)
  4. **Relevant additional information** (research experience, publications, projects, etc.)
- Committee builds pre-nomination list of top candidates for interview
[Link](https://www.scholarshiptab.com/scholarships/2026-erasmus-mundus-neurodata-scholarship)

**Round 2 — Interview:**
- Face-to-face interview via Internet for shortlisted candidates (early March 2026)
- Interview assesses motivation, fit, research interests, understanding of interdisciplinary field
[Link](https://www.neurodata-master.org/online-application)

**Final Selection:**
- Candidates assigned to tracks (committee considers preferences but doesn't guarantee first choice)
- Final nomination list + reserve list prepared
- Lists sent to EACEA (European Education and Culture Executive Agency) for approval
- Administrative Board validates grant attribution
- Students can request track change during Year 1; Administrative Board decides (decision is final)
[Link](https://www.jyu.fi/en/study-with-us/erasmus-mundus-joint-masters-degree-programme-in-brain-and-data-science-master-of-arts-2-yrs-2026)

### Notes (rolling admissions, interviews, etc.):
- **NOT rolling admissions**: Single deadline (Feb 5, 2026), batch processing
- **Paper or email applications NOT accepted**: Must use online portal
- **Track preference**: During application, rank three partner institutions for Year 2 mobility (not guaranteed to receive first choice)
- **Highly selective**: "Due to limited places and high number of applications, Selection Committee has to be very selective"
- **Reserve list mechanism**: If nominated student withdraws, next person from reserve list moves up
- **Contact for appeals**: If believe application not processed properly, write to neuro.data@biu.ac.il
[Link](https://www.neurodata-master.org/online-application)

## 10) Risks, Ambiguities, and To-Confirm Checklist

### Risks:

1. **Track assignment not guaranteed**: Students rank 3 track preferences but may not receive first choice. Committee assigns tracks considering "preferences and need to divide applicants across all tracks homogenously." **Mitigation**: In motivation letter and interview, express genuine interest in multiple tracks and explain why 2-3 would support your goals.

2. **High selectivity (top 10%)**: Program explicitly seeks "outstanding grades (roughly top 10% of your class)." If your GPA is strong but not top 10%, you may face uphill battle. **Mitigation**: Compensate with exceptional research experience, publications, strong references, compelling motivation.

3. **Limited scholarships (10-15) vs. high international demand**: Recent cohort (2025) had 24 students from 14 countries, suggesting many applicants. Scholarship competition is fierce. **Mitigation**: Also explore self-funded route with potential Bar-Ilan tuition waiver + partner university scholarships.

4. **Placement exam failure requires extra coursework in Semester 1**: If you don't pass math, Python, or neuroscience placement exams, you take prerequisites ALONGSIDE standard curriculum. **Mitigation**: Prepare intensively before program starts; program provides this as safety net, not obstacle.

5. **Living across multiple countries (visa complexity, cultural adjustment, moving costs)**: Mobility is enriching but logistically complex. **Mitigation**: Budget time and money for 2-3 international moves; Erasmus scholarship covers visa costs; program provides "support in visa application procedures."

6. **2026-2027 is final cohort of current 4-edition cycle**: After this cohort, program may seek renewal or end. Long-term alumni network may be smaller than established programs. **Note**: Program funded through 2026-2027; future editions would require new EU funding approval.

1. **Exact course titles and syllabi not published**: Program structure described generally ("core courses in neuroscience and data science") but specific module names, ECTS breakdowns, and detailed syllabi not readily available online. **To confirm**: Email neuro.data@biu.ac.il requesting sample curriculum or syllabi.

2. **Self-funded tuition for Year 2**: €10,000/year mentioned for JYU; unclear if all partner universities charge same or different amounts. **To confirm**: Check individual university pages or email program coordinator.

3. **Cost of living estimates per city**: No consolidated cost-of-living guidance found. Living costs in Tel Aviv vs. Lisbon vs. Jyväskylä vary significantly. **To confirm**: Research Numbeo or similar cost-of-living databases for each city (Ramat Gan, Lisbon, Padua, Amsterdam, Jyväskylä).

5. **Employment statistics**: No published placement rates or salary data for graduates (program too new; first cohort graduated ~2024-2025). **To confirm**: Request alumni contact information to inquire about outcomes, or check LinkedIn for recent graduates.

6. **Language requirements for non-scholarship holders**: Clear for scholarship applicants; less clear if self-funded students have different requirements. **Assumption**: Same B2 English requirement applies to all, but confirm if applying self-funded.
