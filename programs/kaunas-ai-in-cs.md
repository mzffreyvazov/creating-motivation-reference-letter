## 1) Executive Summary (5–8 bullets)

- **2-year, full-time MSc** (120 ECTS) awarded by the Faculty of Informatics; degree title is *Master of Computing*; taught in English (evening format, blending on-campus and distance learning — compatible with part-time work).
- The programme trains graduates to build and research advanced AI systems covering **computer vision, speech recognition, semantic analysis, generative AI, and autonomous systems**, with independent research capacity as a stated goal.
- **Single learning pathway** is listed: *Interdisciplinary competence (MA+)* — students may bolt on a secondary competence area (data analytics, data security, project management, computer visualization, challenge-based innovation, etc.) alongside core AI modules.
- Admission score is **90% GPA + 10% motivation letter/interview**; research activities yield up to 2 extra points, making a strong academic record and demonstrated research interest the primary selection levers.
- **Tuition: ~5,641 EUR/year** (Informatics field group); full or partial tuition fee waivers are available automatically to all international applicants based on GPA and motivation; **Lithuanian state scholarships** (592 EUR/month + tuition) are available to Azerbaijani nationals — this is a major funding hook.
- **Application deadline: 1 June 2026**; Lithuanian state scholarship deadline is **20 April 2026** (earlier!).
- KTU runs a dedicated **AI Excellence Centre** and an HPC cluster; research groups in XAI, computer vision in healthcare, IoT/smart systems, and heuristic optimisation directly feed the programme.
- KTU is ranked **243rd in QS Europe 2026** (top 25% of European universities) and is Lithuania's highest-ranked technical university by THE and QS metrics.

## 2) What the Program Optimizes For (Selection Signals)

- **Stated values:**
  - *Technological innovation for societal impact* — the official description frames the programme as "transforming society and tackling global challenges."
  - *Independent research capacity* — learning outcomes explicitly include conducting independent research, not just applying existing tools.
  - *Industry collaboration* — applied projects with business partners and public-sector organisations are embedded in the curriculum; guest practitioners deliver lectures.
  - *Interdisciplinarity* — the MA+ pathway is marketed as a distinguishing feature, encouraging cross-domain expertise.
  - *Flexible, lifelong-compatible learning* — evening schedule (17:30–21:00) and blended delivery are highlighted as intentional design choices.

- **Ideal candidate signals (from programme description and scoring rubric):**
  - Strong GPA in an informatics/engineering/maths bachelor's (minimum 60%; GPA carries 0.9 weight).
  - Background in informatics, informatics engineering, or mathematics (≥30 credits required).
  - Evidence of research or scientific activity (publications, conference presentations, student science societies) — worth up to 2 bonus points.
  - Motivation that connects to specific AI applications (healthcare, autonomous systems, XAI) or to research careers reads best given faculty research focus.
  - Work experience in the field (+0.5 pts); international study experience (+0.5 pts); honours diploma (+0.5 pts).

- **Evidence:** [Programme admission section](https://admissions.ktu.edu/programme/m-artificial-intelligence-in-computer-science/#admission-requirements), [Score structure](https://admissions.ktu.edu/master/#entry-score)

## 3) Curriculum & Academic Experience

- **Structure:** 2 years full-time / 120 ECTS. Blended and evening delivery. Semesters 1–3 cover coursework + rolling research projects; Semester 4 is entirely the **30-credit Master's Final Project** (thesis).

- **Core modules (non-elective, by semester):**

| Semester | Module | Credits |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Computational Intelligence and Decision Making | 6 |
| 1 | Distributed Systems and Algorithms | 6 |
| 1 | Image Processing and Computer Vision | 6 |
| 1 | Research Project 1 | 6 |
| 2 | Explainable Artificial Intelligence | 6 |
| 2 | Generative Artificial Intelligence | 6 |
| 2 | Optimisation Techniques and Algorithms | 6 |
| 2 | Research Project 2 | 6 |
| 3 | Information Technology Project Management | 6 |
| 3 | Research Project 3 | 6 |
| 4 | Master's Degree Final Project | 30 |

- **Competence electives (6 cr. per semester, pick one per semester from field courses):** Options include *Cloud Computing Services*, *Digital Image and Sound Processing*, *Approximation and Modelling of 3D objects*, *Component Based Software System Design*, *Technologies for Mobile Devices*, *Virtual Reality Modelling*.

- **MA+ interdisciplinary electives (6 cr. per semester, optional second-track):** Available tracks include **Data Analytics** (Data Mining, Multivariate Statistical Models, Time Series Analysis), **Data Security** (Cryptographic Systems, Data Security Technologies, Blockchain), **Project Management**, **Computer Visualization Technologies**, **Control Technologies** (including *AI in Robotics*), **Challenge-Based Innovations**, **Management**, and several language tracks.

- **Semester 3 — Applied Research elective (12 cr.):** Students choose between an *Applied Research Project* (industry-partnered) or a *Scientific Internship* — this is the key practical/research immersion component before the thesis.

- **Thesis/Capstone:** The 30-credit Master's Final Project in Semester 4 is on-campus. No details on external examiner involvement were published; learning outcomes register is at [AIKOS](https://www.aikos.smm.lt/en/StudyProgramm/_layouts/15/Asw.Aikos.RegisterSearch/ObjectFormResult.aspx?o=PROG&f=ProgEn&key=4529&pt=of&ctx_sr=5Ap4z%2bWIEVrI04xeoygUSHetiaQ%3d).

- **Distinctive elements:**
  - **Three consecutive Research Projects** (Semesters 1–3) before the thesis — unusually early and sustained research scaffolding.
  - **XAI and Generative AI** are dedicated core modules, not electives — reflecting contemporary industry priorities.
  - Evening/blended delivery enables full-time employment alongside study.
  - HPC access provided for computationally intensive projects.
  - Exchange semester options with Tallinn University of Technology, University of Bologna, Stockholm University, University of Deusto, and Gachon University.

- **Evidence:** [Full curriculum](https://admissions.ktu.edu/programme/m-artificial-intelligence-in-computer-science/)

## 4) Research Ecosystem (Labs/Groups/Faculty)

- **KTU Artificial Intelligence Excellence Centre** — coordinates university-wide AI strategy; runs HPC services; explicitly focuses on nurturing postgraduate researchers; applied work in disease diagnostics, health monitoring, intelligent control, and predictive models. Led by Dr. Agnė Paulauskaitė-Tarasevičienė. [Link](https://aicentre.ktu.edu/)

- **Explainable AI (XAI) Research Group** (Prof. Dr. A. Paulauskaitė-Tarasevičienė) — develops robust model interpretation algorithms, bias detection, adversarial robustness, applied in health, manufacturing, cybersecurity, and agriculture. Directly mirrors the core Semester 2 XAI module. [Link](https://fi.ktu.edu/research/)

- **Computer Vision and Biomedical Signals Analysis** (Prof. Dr. R. Maskeliūnas & Prof. Dr. R. Damaševičius) — AI-driven diagnostics for Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, cancer detection, and posture analysis. Strong intersection of computer vision and healthcare AI. [Link](https://fi.ktu.edu/research/)

- **Heuristic & Metaheuristic Algorithms for Multidisciplinary Applications** (Prof. Dr. G. Palubeckis) — optimisation algorithms for combinatorial problems in manufacturing, transport, agriculture, and healthcare. Feeds directly into the Optimisation Techniques core module. [Link](https://fi.ktu.edu/research/)

- **Semantics and Knowledge-Based Engineering of Information Systems** (Prof. Dr. R. Butleris) — machine learning, NLP, semantic analysis, and knowledge modelling for information systems. Relevant for semantic analysis applications listed in the programme description. [Link](https://fi.ktu.edu/research/)

- **Multimodal Human-Computer Interfaces** (Prof. Dr. T. Blažauskas) — mixed reality, body tracking, haptics, and AI for healthcare, rehabilitation, and education. Overlaps with the MA+ Computer Visualization track. [Link](https://fi.ktu.edu/research/)

- **Centre of Real Time Computer Systems** — IoT and smart environments research; international collaboration with Aalborg, London City, and Darmstadt universities. Relevant for autonomous systems and edge AI applications. [Link](https://compsystems.ktu.edu)

- **Centre of Information Systems Design Technologies (CISDT)** — applied research and feasibility studies in informatics engineering. Provides a vehicle for applied research projects and industry consultancy. [Link](https://fi.ktu.edu/cisdt/)

## 5) Admissions & Eligibility (Hard Requirements)

- **Academic prerequisite:** Bachelor's degree in engineering sciences, technology, mathematical sciences, computer sciences, or physical sciences **and** ≥ 30 ECTS credits in informatics, informatics engineering, or mathematics. Minimum GPA of 60% (on the Lithuanian scale after conversion). [Link](https://admissions.ktu.edu/programme/m-artificial-intelligence-in-computer-science/#admission-requirements)

- **Language requirement:** IELTS ≥ 6.0 (all components ≥ 5.5), TOEFL iBT ≥ 85, or CEFR ≥ C1. If prior degree was fully taught in English and documented on the transcript, an additional certificate may be waived — KTU may also conduct a free online English test for applicants without a certificate. [Link](https://admissions.ktu.edu/master/#minimum-requirements)

- **Required documents (submitted online at apply.ktu.edu):**
  1. Passport / EU ID
  2. Bachelor's diploma + official transcript (with supplement)
  3. Language certificate (if applicable)
  4. Proof of research/scientific activities (optional but adds bonus points)
  5. Proof of work experience (optional, +0.5 pts if ≥1 year in field)
  6. Name-change documentation if applicable
  7. Apostille legalisation for documents from non-EU countries (Azerbaijan is non-EU → Apostille required from Azerbaijani Ministry of Foreign Affairs)

- **Motivation assessment:** Mandatory for all MSc applicants. Format for this programme is **motivation letter + online interview** (exact format is confirmed per-programme by the faculty committee). Carries 0.1 weight in entry score. [Link](https://admissions.ktu.edu/wp-content/uploads/sites/299/2024/04/GUIDELINES-FOR-THE-MOTIVATION-ASSESSMENT_EN.pdf)

- **Academic recognition:** KTU performs its own recognition of foreign qualifications; SKVC certificate not required before application but may be needed later. [Link](https://admissions.ktu.edu/academic-recognition/)

- **Application fee:** Non-refundable fee payable at time of application (amount not stated on fetched pages; confirm with international@ktu.lt).

## 6) Application Process & Deadlines

| Event | Date |
|---|---|
| Application window opens | 15 October 2025 |
| **Lithuanian State Scholarship deadline** | **20 April 2026, 4:00 PM Vilnius time** |
| **General application + KTU tuition waiver deadline** | **1 June 2026** |
| Start of studies | 1 September 2026 |
| KTU scholarship results announced | Mid-July 2026 |
| Lithuanian state scholarship results | No later than 15 June 2026 |

- **Evaluation process:** All applications are assessed by faculty admission committees. Entry score = 0.9 × GPA + 0.1 × motivation assessment (10-point scale) + up to 4 bonus points. [Link](https://admissions.ktu.edu/master/#entry-score)

- **Motivation assessment** (letter + possible interview) is mandatory; applicants who skip it cannot compete. Research activity proof is optional but can add up to 2 points.

- **Rolling / continuous:** Applications are reviewed within 2–3 working days of fee payment. Admission is rolling during the open window but scholarship pools are limited — earlier applications improve scholarship odds.

- **No spring intake** — September intake only.

- **Tuition:** ~5,641 EUR/year (confirmed on programme page for informatics field; prior year general table showed 5,538 EUR for informatics — the programme page figure of 5,641 EUR appears to be the 2025/26 confirmed rate). A candidate with interest in responsible AI, bias mitigation, or AI in regulated domains (healthcare, finance) can frame this as a direct match.
   **Evidence:** [Semester 2 curriculum](https://admissions.ktu.edu/programme/m-artificial-intelligence-in-computer-science/)

## 10) Risks, Ambiguities, and To-Confirm Checklist

- **[ ] Application fee amount not stated** on any fetched official page. Confirm with international@ktu.lt before applying.

- **[ ] Tuition figure discrepancy:** The programme page states 5,641 EUR/year; the general master's tuition table (which appears to reflect 2025/26 rates) shows 5,538 EUR for informatics. The programme page figure is likely updated — confirm with admissions before budgeting.

- **[ ] Motivation assessment format:** The general rules say assessment is either a letter or an interview depending on the programme. The programme page for this MSc lists "motivation letter and online interview" (score structure box). Confirm with Faculty of Informatics whether both are required or one is sufficient.

- **[ ] GPA conversion:** Azerbaijan operates a 5-point or 100-point scale depending on institution. KTU converts using SKVC guidelines. Obtain an official grade conversion table or contact international@ktu.lt to check how Azerbaijani transcripts are treated.

- **[ ] Apostille requirement:** Azerbaijan is a Hague Convention signatory (joined 2004), so Apostille is the correct legalisation path. Confirm this has not changed and allow adequate time for MFA processing.

- **[ ] Lithuanian State Scholarship requires KTU acceptance letter first.** If still a bachelor's student at application time, a transcript of academic record is accepted in lieu of diploma. Clarify timeline: acceptance letter must be issued before 20 April 2026 scholarship deadline — apply to KTU as early as possible (October 2025).

- **[ ] "Study type: evening-time, blended learning" vs "full-time studies."** Both descriptors appear on the programme page. Confirm with faculty whether this affects eligibility for Lithuanian state scholarship (which requires full-time study) or visa/TRP applications.

- **[ ] No published employer placement statistics.** If needed for the motivation letter, request graduate employment data from if@ktu.lt.

- **[ ] Research group membership for students.** Programme page says modules are taught by researchers; confirm whether master's students can formally join research groups or only participate through project modules.
