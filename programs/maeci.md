## 1) Executive Summary (8 bullets)

- MAECI offers fully funded scholarships under Italian Law 288/55, explicitly designed to promote **international cultural, scientific, and technological cooperation**, spread Italian language and culture, and support Italy's global economic standing — the three pillars that must echo in every motivation letter.
- The **financial package** for 2026/27 is **€10,800 total** (paid in 3 instalments directly to the grantee's Italian bank account), full tuition/enrollment fee exemption (university-dependent), and a MAECI-contracted collective health and accident insurance policy for the duration of the grant.
- The grant is structured as **9 months** for Master's programs. Renewal for Year 2 is possible upon demonstrating satisfactory academic progress (minimum 15 CFU/ECTS earned), making it effectively a 2-year funding vehicle if performance criteria are met.
- **Selection is handled by the Italian Diplomatic Mission in the applicant's country** (i.e., the Italian Embassy in Baku for Azerbaijani applicants). The merit ranking is published on the Embassy website and is final and unquestionable.
- **Evaluation criteria (confirmed):** academic merit (CV and transcripts), personal motivation (motivation letter), alignment with the scholarship's objectives of international cooperation, and potential bilateral impact. The motivation letter is explicitly one of the **critical deciding factors** in the selection process.
- The motivation letter is **capped at 500 words** (per the official Study in Italy FAQ) and must cover: personal story, educational background, academic interests, reasons for choosing Italy, and the purpose and goals of the study project. Every word counts.
- **Eligibility hard limits for Master's applicants:** must hold a Bachelor's degree; must not exceed **28 years of age** by 26 March 2026; must be residing **outside Italy**; must not have previously obtained a degree from an Italian institution; must not have held a MAECI research scholarship in the last 5 academic years; must enroll in Year 1 of the chosen program.
- The scholarship is **incompatible with other Italian government/public grants** but can coexist with Erasmus/Erasmus+ and internationally funded programs, as long as studies remain on Italian territory.

- **Selection criteria (confirmed from multiple sources):** academic merit, alignment with program objectives, **potential impact on bilateral cooperation** (Italy ↔ Azerbaijan), and overall application quality.
- **What the motivation letter must answer (official FAQ guidance):**

  4. The purpose and goals of your study project

Generic ambition is insufficient — specificity of purpose is rewarded.
- **Ideal candidate profile:** Academically strong (grades are primary filter); intellectually serious about the field; frames their goals in terms of what they will take back to Azerbaijan, not just what Italy will give them; demonstrates genuine engagement with Italy and the Italian academic ecosystem beyond surface-level interest.

## 3) Scholarship Structure & Academic Context

- **Grant duration:** 9 months (Master's Year 1). Renewable for Year 2 upon satisfactory academic progress (≥15 CFU/ECTS passed). Studies must take place on Italian territory — no online-only programs.
- **Financial breakdown:** Total €10,800 disbursed in 3 instalments into a local Italian bank account:
  - 1st instalment (approx. €4,500): after arrival and completion of enrollment
  - 2nd instalment (approx. €2,500): upon confirmation of enrollment and course attendance (typically February)
  - 3rd instalment (approx. €2,000): upon satisfactory academic performance
  - *Note: Some sources cite €9,000 total — the €10,800 figure is confirmed by the official call text. Verify the exact amount in the 2026/27 Call PDF at [studyinitaly.esteri.it](https://studyinitaly.esteri.it/ListaBandi).*
- **Tuition:** Universities may waive tuition/enrollment fees for MAECI grantees under their autonomy regulations — this is **not automatic** and varies by institution. Sapienza is expected to waive fees for MAECI holders; however, a **regional tax of ~€140 and stamp duty of ~€16** are always payable by the student regardless of scholarship status.
- **Health insurance:** MAECI arranges collective health/accident insurance for the full grant duration. Pre-existing conditions and dental care are excluded. This removes a major financial burden.
- **Chosen program for scholarship (Sapienza MSc Data Science):**
  - 2-year, 120 ECTS, fully English-taught Laurea Magistrale (class LM-Data)
  - Jointly delivered by 4 departments: Computer Science (DI), Engineering (DIAG), Telecommunications (DIET), Statistics (DSS)
  - Culminates in a 24-ECTS thesis with academic or industrial supervisor
  - Lab-based, research-oriented, with international cohort
- **Evidence:** [Scholarships.af — MAECI grant details](https://scholarships.af/opportunity/italy-government-scholarships-for-international-students/); [GyanDhan — instalment breakdown](https://www.gyandhan.com/scholarships/maeci-scholarship); [Sapienza Data Science course home](https://corsidilaurea.uniroma1.it/en/course/33519)

## 5) Admissions & Eligibility (Hard Requirements)

### MAECI Scholarship (Primary)
- **Nationality/Residency:** Azerbaijani citizen residing outside Italy. Azerbaijan must appear on the current [List of Eligible Countries](https://studyinitaly.esteri.it/ListaBandi). — *Confirm before proceeding.*
- **Age:** Must not exceed **28 years of age** by 26 March 2026. [Scholarships.af](https://scholarships.af/opportunity/italy-government-scholarships-for-international-students/)
- **Academic qualification:** Bachelor's degree (or equivalent foreign qualification) required for Master's-level application. [MAECI official page](https://www.esteri.it/en/opportunita/borse-di-studio/per-cittadini-stranieri/borsestudio_stranieri/)
- **Language:** For English-taught programs, **B2 English (CEFR minimum)**. Accepted evidence: recognized language certificate (IELTS ≥6.0 / TOEFL iBT ≥80), OR proof that prior degree was entirely English-taught, OR self-declaration for applicants from English-speaking countries. [etech.pk — language requirements section](https://etech.pk/italian-government-scholarship-2026-2027)
- **Disqualifying conditions:** Currently enrolled at an Italian institution; previously obtained Italian degree; held a MAECI research scholarship in 2021/22–2025/26; applying for Year 2+ entry (except renewals); employee of Italian Public Administration or their first-degree descendant. [DAAD Scholarships — disqualifying conditions](https://daadscholarship.com/italy-fully-funded-maeci-scholarships-2026-2027-open/)
- **Incompatibility:** Cannot be held simultaneously with other Italian government/public institution grants. Compatible with Erasmus+. [InterMaths / MAECI compatibility note](https://www.intermaths.eu/erasmus-mundus/apply)

### Sapienza MSc Data Science (Secondary — for enrollment)
- **Bachelor's degree** in CS (L-31), IT Engineering (L-8), Statistics (L-41), or related field with at least **90 ECTS** in relevant subject areas.
- **No entrance exam** — requirements verification and transcript assessment only.
- **English B2** — same as above.
- **Pre-selection window:** 22 December 2025 – **15 May 2026** via MoveIn portal. [Sapienza Admissions](https://www.uniroma1.it/en/admissions)
- **Required documents for Sapienza:** Valid ID/passport; academic transcripts; language certificate; personal statement; €30 application fee.

## 7) Funding / Tuition / Cost

- **Total grant:** €10,800 (three instalments into Italian bank account). Effective monthly equivalent: ~€900/month over 9 months. [Official MAECI call text via opportunitiesforafricans.com](https://www.opportunitiesforafricans.com/italian-government-scholarships-2026-2027/)
- **Tuition waiver:** Expected at Sapienza for MAECI holders, but **not guaranteed by MAECI** — confirm directly with Sapienza. Regional tax (~€140) and stamp duty (~€16) are always due. [GyanDhan](https://www.gyandhan.com/scholarships/maeci-scholarship)
- **Health insurance:** Full cover for duration of grant (illness and accidents; excludes pre-existing conditions and dental). Significant value — typical private health insurance in Italy costs €600–€1,200/year.
- **Estimated monthly costs in Rome:**
  - Accommodation: €500–€650 (shared apartment near Sapienza)
  - Food, transport, personal: €350–€500
  - **Total: ~€850–€1,150/month**
  - The €900 stipend covers or nearly covers living costs; gap is modest.
- **Scholarship incompatibility:** Cannot combine with DSU (regional need-based) or other Italian public grants. Verify DiSCo Lazio compatibility before accepting any secondary awards.

## 9) Motivation Letter Fit Hooks (12 Hooks — MAECI-First)

Remember: **500-word hard cap**. Every hook should be expressed in 1–3 sentences in the actual letter.

MAECI funds scholars explicitly to foster Italy–Azerbaijan scientific and technological cooperation. The motivation letter must include a concrete sentence about what the applicant will bring back to Azerbaijan — not just what Italy will provide. Frame the scholarship as an investment with a bilateral return.
**Why it matters:** This is the single most confirmed selection signal. The committee evaluates "potential impact on bilateral cooperation" as a primary criterion.
