## 1) Executive Summary (5–8 bullets)

- A 4-semester (2-year), 120 ECTS credit, fully English-taught MSc at NIK — Óbuda's IT faculty — training graduates to understand, transform, model, and draw conclusions from complex data sets, including readiness for doctoral-level study.
- The program has **no named specialisation tracks**; it covers a broad, integrated curriculum spanning statistics, machine learning, data ethics, IT security, and multivariate analysis.
- Admission requires a BSc-level background with a defined 65-credit prerequisite profile in relevant subjects; at least 30 of those must be completed at enrolment, with the remainder allowed as makeup credits during the MSc.
- Tuition is **€2,500/semester (EU/EEA)** or **€3,500/semester (non-EEA)**; full Stipendium Hungaricum scholarships (tuition + stipend + accommodation) are available for eligible nationals. For 2025/26 intake, the SH deadline was 15 January 2026 — confirm the equivalent date for 2026/27 intake when announced.
- NIK's research portfolio directly supporting the program includes Applied Machine Learning, Generative AI, eXplainable AI, Biomedical Affective Computing, High Performance Computing, and Health Economics/HTA groups.
- Óbuda University is ranked **601–800 globally (THE 2026)**, top 350 young universities globally, and considered Hungary's leading technical university in IT/Engineering by THE subject rankings.
- The university operates an **Innovation Ecosystem** with a Venture Capital arm, Initium Venture Labs, and an AI Campus — signalling strong industry-to-research linkages.
- Graduates are expected to work in R&D, data-intensive industry, financial technology, software engineering, and research institutions across Hungary and internationally.

## 3) Curriculum & Academic Experience

- **Structure:** 4 semesters, 120 ECTS credits total, part-time course format listed (confirm full-time availability at enrolment). Degree requires a final certificate plus a B2 or higher language exam in any foreign language (separate from English proficiency at entry).
- **Credit areas (official breakdown):**
  - Basic theoretical knowledge: 15–25 credits
  - Practical basic knowledge: 15–25 credits
  - Specific knowledge: 40–60 credits
  - Optional subjects + Thesis: 30–50 credits

- **Core knowledge domains (from competence framework — representative, not a module list):**
  - Data types, representations, transformation and optimisation
  - Multivariate statistics and machine learning
  - Large-scale data storage, processing, and visualisation
  - Data science ethics, legal/regulatory aspects, IT security
  - Application domains and problem-solving in multidisciplinary environments

- **Electives:** The program structure includes optional subjects within the 30–50 credit flexible block. Specific elective module titles are not published on the English-language pages — the NIK course descriptions system at [targyleirasok](https://nik.uni-obuda.hu/targyleirasok/en/course-descriptions/) may contain them; current thesis topics are listed at [temaleadas](https://nik.uni-obuda.hu/temaleadas/en/current-topics/).

- **Thesis/Capstone:** The thesis defence is a formal component of the final exam, taken on the same day as oral subject exams. The final grade is the average of the thesis grade and the oral exam grades: F = (Th + S1 + S2 + … + Sm) / (1 + m). Thesis topics are publicly listed and supervised by faculty from NIK research groups.

- **Practical components:** A mandatory professional internship (práctica) of at least 6 weeks / 240 work hours is required. Talented MSc students may join NIK research groups alongside doctoral students, and the Scientific Students' Association (TDK) provides a forum for presenting research results each semester.

- **Distinctive elements:**
  - Prerequisite credit flexibility: up to 35 of the 65 prerequisite BSc credits can be earned *during* the MSc as additional credits outside the core curriculum — lowering the entry barrier for students with adjacent backgrounds.
  - Direct doctoral pathway: program explicitly prepares graduates to continue at PhD level; the Doctoral School of Applied Informatics and Applied Mathematics is co-located.
  - AI Campus: Óbuda has a dedicated AI Campus (ai.uni-obuda.hu) operating alongside the main campus, indicating institutional investment in AI infrastructure.

- **Evidence (links):** [Program structure & competences](https://nik.uni-obuda.hu/en/data-science-msc-2/), [NIK thesis topics](https://nik.uni-obuda.hu/temaleadas/en/current-topics/), [NIK course descriptions](https://nik.uni-obuda.hu/targyleirasok/en/course-descriptions/)

## 4) Research Ecosystem (Labs/Groups/Faculty)

All groups are at NIK or the co-located University Research and Innovation Centre (URIC / EKIK). MSc students may join these groups.

- **Applied Machine Learning group** — Core relevance: supervised, unsupervised and reinforcement learning methods directly forming the backbone of the MSc curriculum. [NIK Research](https://nik.uni-obuda.hu/en/research/)
- **Generative Artificial Intelligence group** — Works on generative models (LLMs, diffusion, etc.); highly current and letter-relevant for framing research interests. [NIK Research](https://nik.uni-obuda.hu/en/research/)
- **eXplainable AI (XAI) group** — Focuses on interpretability and trust in AI systems; aligns with the program's emphasis on data ethics and IT security. [NIK Research](https://nik.uni-obuda.hu/en/research/)
- **Biomedical Affective Computing (BioMAC)** — Applies ML/data science to biomedical signals and affective states; relevant for interdisciplinary data science applications in health. [NIK Research](https://nik.uni-obuda.hu/en/research/)
- **High Performance Computing group** — Covers large-scale data processing infrastructure, directly supporting the "big data" curriculum strand. [NIK Research](https://nik.uni-obuda.hu/en/research/)
- **Health Economics and Health Technology Assessment** — Applies statistical and data analysis methods to healthcare policy; strong example of data science in real-world multidisciplinary contexts. [NIK Research](https://nik.uni-obuda.hu/en/research/)
- **Biostatistical analysis of public health problems** — Another medical-data intersection group; useful for candidates with a quantitative/health-data interest. [NIK Research](https://nik.uni-obuda.hu/en/research/)
- **Doctoral School of Applied Informatics and Applied Mathematics** — The PhD school co-located with NIK focusing on AI, cyber-physical systems, robotics, computational engineering; the natural continuation path after the MSc. [Doctoral School](https://aidi.uni-obuda.hu/en)
- **University Research and Innovation Centre (URIC/EKIK)** — Cross-faculty applied research hub in robotics, health informatics, and R&D; NIK research groups collaborate through it. [URIC](https://ekik.uni-obuda.hu/)

## 5) Admissions & Eligibility (Hard Requirements)

- **Academic (degree):** A relevant BSc or equivalent from a recognised institution. [University graduate admissions](https://uni-obuda.hu/graduate-programs/)
- **Prerequisite credits:** 65 credits from BSc studies in relevant subjects are required for application. At least 30 must be completed at the time of enrolment; the remaining ≤35 may be completed during the MSc as supplementary credits. [Program page](https://nik.uni-obuda.hu/en/data-science-msc/)
  - *Note: The specific breakdown of which subjects qualify for the 65 credits is linked from the program page but the full list is not reproduced on the English page — confirm with NIK coordinator at international@nik.uni-obuda.hu.*
- **Language (MSc):** TOEFL iBT 72 / IELTS 5.5 / Cambridge FCE / ECL B2 / Euroexam B2 / PTE General B2. Duolingo is NOT accepted. Waived if prior degree was fully taught in English and diploma was issued in English. [Required Documents](https://uni-obuda.hu/required-documents/)
- **Degree for graduation:** In addition to passing exams, a B2 or above language exam in *any* foreign language is required for the degree certificate. [Program page](https://nik.uni-obuda.hu/en/data-science-msc-2/)
- **Documents (all applicants):** Copy of passport, motivation letter, CV, proof of English proficiency. [Required Documents](https://uni-obuda.hu/required-documents/)
- **Documents (MSc-specific):** Official transcripts of completed degrees/diplomas with notarised English translation; **two letters of recommendation**. [Required Documents](https://uni-obuda.hu/required-documents/)
- **Application fee:** €150 (non-refundable, paid at application; separate from tuition). [Tuition fees](https://uni-obuda.hu/tuition-fees/)
- **No references to GPA minimum** were found on official English pages — admission appears based on academic background review and a technical interview (standard for MSc programs at Óbuda). Confirm with the faculty.

## 7) Funding / Tuition / Cost

- **Tuition (Data Science MSc — no specialisation):**
  - EU/EEA students: **€2,500/semester** (≈ €10,000 total over 4 semesters)
  - Non-EEA students: **€3,500/semester** (≈ €14,000 total)
  - [Official tuition table](https://uni-obuda.hu/tuition-fees/)

- **Stipendium Hungaricum (primary scholarship route for international non-EEA students):**
  - Coverage: full tuition waiver + monthly stipend of HUF 43,700 (≈ €110–115/month at current rates) for BSc/MSc + accommodation support + health insurance.
  - Eligibility: citizens of partner countries (Azerbaijan is a Stipendium Hungaricum partner country — confirm quota availability).
  - Apply through the national sending authority in the student's country + Tempus Public Foundation portal.
  - Deadline for 2025/26 was 15 January 2026. Watch for 2026/27 announcement.
  - [Óbuda SH page](https://uni-obuda.hu/stipendium-hungaricum/), [National SH portal](https://stipendiumhungaricum.hu/)

- **Other scholarships:**
  - National Higher Education Scholarship (NFÖH): up to HUF 40,000/month for top academic performers; requires university nomination.
  - Pannónia Scholarship Programme: mobility grants for 2–5 month exchanges/traineeships at partner universities globally.
  - University Merit Awards: 20–50% tuition reduction based on GPA and interview (for self-financing students).

- **Cost of living (Budapest):** Budapest is significantly more affordable than Western European capitals. A student budget of approximately €600–900/month covers accommodation, food, transport, and personal expenses. The university has dormitories (on-campus) as the most affordable option. [Life in Budapest](https://uni-obuda.hu/life-in-budapest/)
